# FAR Calculations Reference

Step-by-step Floor Area Ratio calculation examples for five distinct site scenarios, followed by
GFA measurement conventions across major jurisdictions and bonus FAR mechanisms. Use this reference
whenever precise FAR calculations are required for a site, district, or development proposal.

---

## Scenario 1: Simple Single-Use Residential

**Site:** 1 hectare (10,000 m2), flat, rectangular, no setback constraints beyond standard.
**Typology:** 4-story perimeter block.
**Use:** 100% residential.

### Calculation

| Step | Parameter | Value | Calculation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Gross site area | 10,000 m2 (1.0 ha) | Given |
| 2 | Street/infrastructure deduction | 28% | Urban grid with 16m-wide streets on all four sides |
| 3 | Net developable area | 7,200 m2 | 10,000 x (1 - 0.28) |
| 4 | Block dimensions | 85m x 85m (approx.) | Within the net area |
| 5 | Building depth | 14m | Double-loaded corridor apartments |
| 6 | Courtyard | 57m x 57m = 3,249 m2 | Block size minus 2x building depth |
| 7 | Building footprint | 85 x 85 - 57 x 57 = 3,976 m2 | Perimeter ring footprint |
| 8 | Site coverage | 39.8% | 3,976 / 10,000 (of gross site) |
| 9 | Number of floors | 4 | Perimeter block, low-mid rise |
| 10 | Ground floor height | 4.0m | Commercial-ready but used as residential |
| 11 | Upper floor height | 3.0m | Residential standard |
| 12 | Building height | 13.0m | 4.0 + (3 x 3.0) |
| 13 | GFA per floor | 3,976 m2 | Same footprint all floors |
| 14 | **Total GFA** | **15,904 m2** | 3,976 x 4 |
| 15 | **FAR** | **1.59** | 15,904 / 10,000 |
| 16 | Efficiency ratio | 0.80 | Walk-up with corridor |
| 17 | Net Internal Area | 12,723 m2 | 15,904 x 0.80 |
| 18 | Average unit size | 80 m2 | 2-bed weighted average |
| 19 | **Dwelling units** | **159 units** | 12,723 / 80 |
| 20 | Household size | 2.4 | Family neighborhood |
| 21 | **Population** | **382 persons** | 159 x 2.4 |
| 22 | **Net density** | **221 DU/ha** | 159 / 0.72 |
| 23 | **Gross density** | **159 DU/ha** | 159 / 1.0 |

### Verification
- Courtyard dimension: 57m (exceeds 21m minimum by a wide margin; excellent daylight)
- Privacy distance across courtyard: 57m (exceeds 22m minimum)
- FAR 1.59 is within the 1.5-3.5 range for courtyard/perimeter blocks
- Coverage 39.8% is moderate; leaves 60% as open space (courtyard + streets)

## Scenario 2: Mixed-Use with Podium Retail

**Site:** 0.5 hectare (5,000 m2), corner lot, transit-adjacent.
**Typology:** 3-story podium + 12-story residential tower.
**Use:** Ground-3 floors commercial/retail podium, floors 4-12 residential tower.

### Calculation

| Step | Parameter | Value | Calculation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Gross site area | 5,000 m2 (0.5 ha) | Given |
| 2 | Street deduction | 0% | Corner lot, streets are external to site boundary |
| 3 | Net developable area | 5,000 m2 | No deductions (individual lot, not district) |
| 4 | Podium footprint | 3,750 m2 | 75% coverage of site |
| 5 | Podium floors | 3 stories | Retail/commercial |
| 6 | Podium floor-to-floor | 4.5m | Retail standard (high ceiling) |
| 7 | Podium GFA | 11,250 m2 | 3,750 x 3 |
| 8 | Tower footprint | 1,000 m2 | 20% of site area (slender tower) |
| 9 | Tower floors | 9 stories | Floors 4-12 (9 residential floors above podium) |
| 10 | Tower floor-to-floor | 3.0m | Residential standard |
| 11 | Tower GFA | 9,000 m2 | 1,000 x 9 |
| 12 | **Total GFA** | **20,250 m2** | 11,250 + 9,000 |
| 13 | **FAR** | **4.05** | 20,250 / 5,000 |
| 14 | **Use split** | **56% commercial, 44% residential** | 11,250 / 20,250 and 9,000 / 20,250 |
| 15 | Residential efficiency | 0.75 | Tower with central core |
| 16 | Residential NIA | 6,750 m2 | 9,000 x 0.75 |
| 17 | Average unit size | 70 m2 | Urban mixed-use (smaller units near transit) |
| 18 | **Dwelling units** | **96 units** | 6,750 / 70 |
| 19 | Household size | 2.1 | Urban location, smaller households |
| 20 | **Population** | **202 persons** | 96 x 2.1 |
| 21 | **Gross density** | **192 DU/ha** | 96 / 0.5 |
| 22 | Commercial efficiency | 0.85 | Retail open plan |
| 23 | Net commercial area | 9,563 m2 | 11,250 x 0.85 |
| 24 | Building height | 40.5m | (3 x 4.5) + (9 x 3.0) = 13.5 + 27.0 |

### Verification
- FAR 4.05 is within the 4.0-12.0 range for tower-podium typology
- Tower floor plate 1,000 m2 is reasonable (within 600-1,500 m2 range)
- Podium coverage 75% is within the 60-80% guideline
- Building height 40.5m (approximately 12 stories) is appropriate for transit-adjacent site

## Scenario 4: Infill Development

**Site:** 2,000 m2 (0.2 ha), irregular L-shaped lot in existing urban fabric.
**Typology:** L-shaped mid-rise apartment building.
**Constraints:** 3m front setback, 5m rear setback, 3m side setbacks, 22m height limit (6 stories).

### Calculation

| Step | Parameter | Value | Calculation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Gross site area | 2,000 m2 | Given (irregular L-shape) |
| 2 | Street deduction | 0% | Infill lot; streets are external |
| 3 | Setback area | 520 m2 | Front (3m x 30m = 90), rear (5m x 25m = 125), sides (3m x various = 305) |
| 4 | Buildable area | 1,480 m2 | 2,000 - 520 (approximate; varies with irregular shape) |
| 5 | Building footprint | 1,050 m2 | L-shaped, fitting within setbacks |
| 6 | Site coverage | 52.5% | 1,050 / 2,000 |
| 7 | Number of floors | 6 | Maximum under height limit |
| 8 | Ground floor height | 4.0m | Commercial |
| 9 | Upper floor height | 3.2m | Residential |
| 10 | Building height | 20.0m | 4.0 + (5 x 3.2) = 20.0m (under 22m limit) |
| 11 | Ground floor GFA | 1,050 m2 | Full footprint |
| 12 | Floors 2-6 GFA | 4,725 m2 | 945 m2 per floor x 5 (reduced footprint upper floors due to setback at top) |
| 13 | **Total GFA** | **5,775 m2** | 1,050 + 4,725 |
| 14 | **FAR** | **2.89** | 5,775 / 2,000 |
| 15 | Ground floor commercial | 1,050 m2 | 100% of ground floor |
| 16 | Residential GFA | 4,725 m2 | Floors 2-6 |
| 17 | Residential efficiency | 0.76 | Mid-rise with central core |
| 18 | Residential NIA | 3,591 m2 | 4,725 x 0.76 |
| 19 | Average unit size | 68 m2 | Urban infill, smaller units |
| 20 | **Dwelling units** | **52 units** | 3,591 / 68 |
| 21 | **Gross density** | **260 DU/ha** | 52 / 0.2 |

### Verification
- FAR 2.89 is achievable with 6 stories at 52.5% coverage
- Building fits within all setbacks
- Height 20.0m is under the 22m limit with margin
- 52 units on 2,000 m2 is efficient infill without being excessive
- Ground-floor commercial activates the street frontage

## GFA Measurement Conventions by Jurisdiction

### United States: BOMA Standard (Building Owners and Managers Association)

**BOMA 2017 (Office) and BOMA 2023 updates** define two key metrics:
- **Rentable Area:** includes usable area plus a pro-rata share of common areas (lobbies, corridors, restrooms, mechanical rooms). This is what tenants pay for.
- **Usable Area:** the actual area occupied by the tenant within their demised premises.

**What counts as GFA for zoning purposes (varies by city):**
- All enclosed floor area measured to outside face of exterior walls: YES
- Below-grade parking levels: typically NO (excluded from FAR)
- Mechanical rooms and plant: varies (NYC excludes mechanical space from zoning floor area under certain conditions; other cities include it)
- Common lobbies and corridors: YES
- Balconies (covered): varies by jurisdiction (NYC counts covered balconies toward floor area; many other cities do not)
- Balconies (uncovered/open): typically NO
- Terraces (uncovered rooftop): NO
- Loading docks: varies
- Residential amenity rooms (gym, pool): varies (some cities exclude community facilities up to a cap)

### United Kingdom: RICS Standards (Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors)

**Gross Internal Area (GIA):**
- Measured to the internal face of the external walls at each floor level
- Includes all internal areas: columns, partitions, stairwells, lift shafts, corridors, plant rooms, toilets, storage, internal car parking
- Excludes: external open balconies, canopies, external open-sided covered areas, voids over main entrance halls (if measurable)
- Used for: building cost calculation, insurance, business rates

**Net Internal Area (NIA):**
- The usable area within a building measured to the internal face of external walls, excluding columns, corridors, stairwells, lift shafts, plant rooms, toilets, reception areas, internal structural walls, and other non-usable space
- Used for: rental valuation, space efficiency calculation
- NIA is typically 70-85% of GIA depending on building type

**For planning/zoning FAR calculation:** UK local planning authorities typically use GIA.

### Singapore: URA (Urban Redevelopment Authority) Gross Floor Area

**URA GFA includes:**
- All covered floor areas measured to the external face of external walls or to the center line of party walls
- Covered linkways and corridors
- Car porches and covered drop-off areas
- Strata lobbies and strata void decks

**URA GFA excludes (subject to approval):**
- Covered car parks (if compliant with parking provision requirements)
- Mechanical and electrical (M&E) spaces (limited to reasonable proportion)
- Bin centers, bicycle parking, and end-of-trip facilities
- Covered public spaces and through-block links (if publicly accessible)
- Void areas (double-height spaces, atriums)
- Balconies (up to a cap; typically bonus GFA for balconies up to certain percentage)
- Sky terraces and roof gardens (under Landscaping for Urban Spaces and High-Rises, LUSH, program)
- Communal planting areas

Singapore's system is notable for using GFA exclusions as an incentive mechanism: by excluding
desirable features (sky gardens, communal spaces, bicycle parking) from GFA, the URA encourages
developers to include them without penalty to their FAR.

### Hong Kong: Buildings Department (BD) Gross Floor Area

**BD GFA includes:**
- All floor areas measured to the external face of external walls
- Corridors, lobbies, staircases, lift shafts
- Clubhouse facilities and management offices
- Canopies and covered projections

**BD GFA concessions (excluded from GFA under certain conditions):**
- Car parking spaces (if meeting transport requirements)
- Loading/unloading areas
- Refuse collection rooms
- Plant rooms and lift machine rooms
- Recreational facilities (up to 5% of total domestic GFA, or 50% of recreational facilities, whichever is greater)
- Balconies (up to a cap per unit)
- Non-structural prefabricated external walls (green building incentive)

**Key difference:** Hong Kong's GFA includes common areas (corridors, lobbies, clubhouses, management offices) that most other jurisdictions exclude. When a Hong Kong developer reports a unit as "800 sq ft," this typically includes the buyer's pro-rata share of common area, meaning the actual usable space may be only 550-650 sq ft. This practice, known as "including common area in the saleable area," was reformed in 2013 with the introduction of Saleable Area (SA) as the mandatory measurement for residential sales, but GFA remains the basis for planning and building control.

## Bonus FAR Mechanisms

Bonus FAR (also called incentive zoning, density bonuses, or development bonuses) allows developers
to build additional floor area beyond the base zoning allowance in exchange for providing public
benefits. The following mechanisms are widely used across jurisdictions.

### Affordable Housing Bonus
- **Mechanism:** Additional FAR granted when a developer includes a specified percentage of affordable or below-market-rate units in the development.
- **Typical bonus:** 10-35% additional FAR for providing 10-20% affordable units.
- **Examples:**
  - New York City Voluntary Inclusionary Housing: up to 33% FAR bonus for 20% of units at 80% AMI
  - San Francisco: density bonus up to 35% for projects with 15-24% affordable units (California State Density Bonus Law)
  - Singapore: additional GFA for public housing components in integrated developments
  - London: density above policy levels permitted where affordable housing provision exceeds 35% (Fast Track Route, London Plan)
- **Calculation example:** Base FAR 4.0 on a 10,000 m2 site = 40,000 m2 GFA. Developer provides 20% affordable units. Bonus: 25% additional FAR = 1.0. Total FAR: 5.0 = 50,000 m2 GFA. Of this, 20% (10,000 m2) is affordable housing.

### Green Building Bonus
- **Mechanism:** Additional FAR granted for achieving specified green building certification levels or incorporating specific sustainable design features.
- **Typical bonus:** 5-15% additional FAR for LEED Gold/Platinum, Green Mark Platinum, or equivalent.
- **Examples:**
  - Singapore Green Mark GFA Incentive Scheme: up to 2% additional GFA for Green Mark Platinum, up to 1% for Green Mark GoldPlus
  - Arlington County, Virginia: up to 0.25 additional FAR for LEED Silver, up to 0.35 for LEED Gold, up to 0.50 for LEED Platinum
  - Hong Kong: GFA concessions for non-structural prefabricated external walls (encouraging better thermal performance)
  - Seattle: up to 4% additional FAR for Living Building Challenge or LEED Platinum
- **Calculation example:** Base FAR 3.0 on a 5,000 m2 site = 15,000 m2 GFA. LEED Platinum achieved. Bonus: 10% additional FAR = 0.3. Total FAR: 3.3 = 16,500 m2 GFA.

### Public Amenity Bonus
- **Mechanism:** Additional FAR granted for providing publicly accessible amenities such as plazas, parks, community facilities, cultural spaces, transit improvements, or pedestrian connections.
- **Typical bonus:** 5-20% additional FAR depending on the type and value of the amenity provided.
- **Examples:**
  - New York City: public plaza bonus (1 sq ft of bonus floor area per 1 sq ft of plaza area, up to 20% bonus under older zoning; reformed in 2007 to emphasize plaza quality)
  - San Francisco: public open space bonus in downtown districts
  - Singapore: community/sports facilities bonus (additional GFA for developers who include public sports or community facilities)
  - Vancouver: community amenity contributions (CACs) negotiated on a site-by-site basis in exchange for rezoning to higher density
  - Tokyo: comprehensive design system allows FAR bonuses up to 1.5x base in exchange for public plazas, transit connections, and seismic improvements
- **Calculation example:** Base FAR 5.0 on a 8,000 m2 site = 40,000 m2 GFA. Developer provides 1,500 m2 public plaza and 500 m2 community room. Bonus: 15% additional FAR = 0.75. Total FAR: 5.75 = 46,000 m2 GFA.

### Heritage Transfer of Development Rights (TDR)
- **Mechanism:** Unused FAR from a heritage or landmark building (which cannot be developed to its full zoning potential due to preservation) is transferred to a development site, allowing the receiving site to exceed its base FAR.
- **Typical transfer:** the full unused FAR of the sending site, subject to receiving site capacity limits.
- **Examples:**
  - New York City: landmarks can transfer unused development rights to adjacent lots or (in special districts) to lots across the street or further. Grand Central Terminal's unused air rights have been transferred multiple times (e.g., One Vanderbilt received transferred development rights)
  - Hong Kong: transfer of plot ratio from heritage sites to development sites within the same district
  - Mumbai: Transferable Development Rights (TDR) issued for heritage preservation, road widening, and slum rehabilitation; TDR can be sold on the open market
  - Many US cities: TDR programs for agricultural land preservation, where development rights from farmland are purchased and applied to urban infill sites
- **Calculation example:** Heritage building on a 2,000 m2 lot in a FAR 10.0 zone. The existing heritage building uses only FAR 3.0 (6,000 m2 GFA). Unused FAR: 7.0 (14,000 m2). This 14,000 m2 of unused development right can be transferred to a receiving site. If the receiving site is 5,000 m2 with base FAR 10.0 (50,000 m2 GFA), it could potentially receive up to 14,000 m2, bringing total GFA to 64,000 m2 (effective FAR 12.8), subject to receiving site capacity limits (structural, infrastructure, daylight, urban design review).

### Combined Bonus Limits
Most jurisdictions cap the total bonus FAR to prevent overdevelopment:
- **Typical combined cap:** 20-50% above base FAR
- **Example:** Base FAR 4.0 with a 40% combined cap. Maximum total FAR = 4.0 x 1.4 = 5.6, regardless of how many individual bonus provisions are invoked.
- Some jurisdictions apply a hard cap on total FAR regardless of bonuses (e.g., NYC has maximum FAR caps by district that cannot be exceeded even with bonuses).

### Best Practice for Bonus FAR Analysis
1. Identify the base FAR from the zoning code.
2. List all applicable bonus provisions and their conditions.
3. Calculate the bonus GFA for each provision.
4. Sum all bonuses and check against the combined cap.
5. Verify that the total development (base + bonus) can be physically accommodated on the site (footprint, height, setbacks, daylight).
6. Confirm that all bonus conditions can be met (affordable units delivered, certification achieved, public amenity constructed and maintained).
7. Factor bonus GFA into the financial pro forma: bonus floor area generates additional revenue but bonus conditions (affordable housing, amenities) impose costs. The net benefit must be positive for the developer to pursue the bonus.
