# Jan Gehl 12 Quality Criteria - Detailed Scoring and Application Guide

This reference provides the complete scoring methodology for Jan Gehl's 12 Quality Criteria, application guidance for each space type, observation methodology based on the Gehl Institute's Public Life Tools, and data collection templates for conducting public life studies.

---

## 1. Scoring Methodology

Each of the 12 criteria is scored on a 1 - 5 scale. The definitions below provide clear benchmarks for each score level. A project should target a minimum score of 3 on every criterion and an average score of 4.0 or higher across all 12.

### PROTECTION

#### Criterion 1: Protection Against Traffic and Accidents

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Completely car-free space. No vehicle access possible during operating hours. Physical barriers on all sides. Zero pedestrian-vehicle conflict. |
| 4 | Vehicles limited to service access at restricted hours only. Robust physical separation (bollards, planters, level changes). Raised crossings at pedestrian grade on any shared surfaces. |
| 3 | Adjacent traffic present but physically separated by curb, bollard, or planter. Some crossing points exist but are controlled (signals, raised tables). Speed limit 30 km/h or below on adjacent streets. |
| 2 | Adjacent traffic at moderate speeds (30 - 50 km/h) with only painted separation. Some uncontrolled pedestrian-vehicle conflict points. Service vehicles cross pedestrian areas during use hours. |
| 1 | Active through-traffic adjacent to or within the space. No physical separation. High-speed traffic (>50 km/h). Frequent vehicle-pedestrian conflicts. |

#### Criterion 2: Protection Against Crime and Violence

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Excellent natural surveillance from surrounding buildings (windows, balconies, active ground floors on 3+ sides). Even lighting 30 - 50 lux throughout. Clear sightlines everywhere. Active use day and night. Diverse user groups present at all hours. |
| 4 | Good surveillance from 2+ sides. Adequate lighting (20 - 30 lux) with few dark spots. Generally clear sightlines with minor obstructions. Active into the evening hours. |
| 3 | Moderate surveillance from at least 1 active edge. Acceptable lighting on main paths but some darker zones. Most sightlines clear. Active during the day, reduced activity in evening. |
| 2 | Limited surveillance; mostly blank walls or inactive edges. Patchy lighting with noticeable dark areas. Some hidden corners or obstructed sightlines. Low activity after dark. |
| 1 | No natural surveillance. Poor or no lighting. Multiple hidden corners, dense shrubs, blind spots. Feels unsafe during the day. Evidence of antisocial behavior (graffiti, vandalism, litter). |

#### Criterion 3: Protection Against Unpleasant Sensory Experiences

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Fully sheltered from wind (< 3 m/s at seating level). Ambient noise < 55 dB. Excellent air quality. Rain shelter available within 20m. No glare, dust, or odor. Comfortable in all weather. |
| 4 | Good wind shelter with occasional gusts. Noise 55 - 60 dB. Clean air. Rain shelter within 30m. Minor sensory issues on rare occasions. |
| 3 | Moderate wind exposure; some unpleasant wind days. Noise 60 - 65 dB (noticeable but tolerable). Adequate air quality. Rain shelter within 50m. Occasional discomfort. |
| 2 | Frequent wind discomfort (> 5 m/s regularly). Noise 65 - 70 dB (difficult to converse). Some air quality issues (traffic exhaust). Limited rain shelter. Regular sensory discomfort. |
| 1 | Severe wind (tunnel effects, sustained > 8 m/s). Noise > 70 dB (unpleasant). Poor air quality. No rain shelter. Dust, glare, or odor problems. Uncomfortable most days. |

### COMFORT

#### Criterion 4: Opportunities to Walk

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | All paths follow desire lines. Excellent surfaces (even, non-slip, well-drained). Fully accessible (no steps on primary routes, max 5% gradient). Path widths 2.5m+ on main routes. Interesting things to see every 50m. Tactile indicators and wayfinding for all users. |
| 4 | Most paths follow desire lines. Good surfaces with minor imperfections. Accessible main routes. Widths 2.0 - 2.5m. Regular interest points. Clear wayfinding. |
| 3 | Paths generally logical but some detours from desire lines. Adequate surfaces. Main routes accessible but some secondary paths have steps or steep gradients. Widths 1.8 - 2.0m. Some interest along the way. |
| 2 | Paths force significant detours. Some poor surfaces (uneven, slippery, puddling). Accessibility barriers on some routes. Narrow widths (< 1.8m) in places. Long stretches with nothing to see. |
| 1 | No logical path network. Desire lines ignored. Poor surfaces throughout. Major accessibility barriers. Narrow or non-existent paths. No visual interest. Confusing navigation. |

#### Criterion 5: Opportunities to Stand and Linger

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Abundant edge conditions: facades with niches, columns, doorways, planters, railings, trees to lean on. Lingering supports at 0.9 - 1.1m throughout. Covered transition zones (arcades, canopies). Multiple "hang-out" spots with activity to watch. |
| 4 | Good edge conditions along 2+ sides. Several lingering supports. Some covered areas. Clear spots for people-watching. |
| 3 | Moderate edge conditions along 1 - 2 sides. Some lingering supports available. Limited covered areas. At least one good people-watching spot. |
| 2 | Limited edges; mostly open space with few lingering supports. No covered transition zones. Few comfortable standing spots. |
| 1 | No edge conditions worth lingering at. Blank walls, dead edges, or completely open. No lingering supports. Nothing to lean on or stand near comfortably. |

#### Criterion 6: Opportunities to Sit

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Abundant and varied seating exceeding minimum standards. Primary seating (benches with backs and armrests) and secondary seating (walls, steps, planters, grass). Moveable chairs available. Seating in sun, shade, wind-shelter, and with views. Social, semi-private, and private seating options. Cafe seating present. |
| 4 | Good quantity of seating meeting minimum standards. Mix of primary and secondary seating. Some sun/shade options. Social and individual options. |
| 3 | Adequate seating at minimum standards. Primarily fixed benches with limited variety. Some sun or shade but not both. Limited orientation options. |
| 2 | Below minimum seating standards. Mostly one type of seating. Poor orientation (facing blank walls, in permanent shade or full sun). Limited comfort (no backs, hostile design). |
| 1 | Almost no seating, or seating that is hostile by design (anti-homeless features, uncomfortable materials, fully exposed). No secondary seating opportunities. |

#### Criterion 7: Opportunities to See

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Clear sightlines 50 - 100m along all main paths and across the primary space. Excellent lighting (30+ lux on gathering areas). Framed views to landmarks, water, sky, or greenery. Elevated viewpoints available. No visual clutter. |
| 4 | Good sightlines across most of the space. Good lighting (20 - 30 lux). Some framed views. Minimal clutter. |
| 3 | Adequate sightlines along main routes. Acceptable lighting on primary paths. Views present but not designed or framed. Some visual clutter. |
| 2 | Obstructed sightlines in several areas. Patchy lighting. No designed views. Significant clutter (signage, overgrown planting, poorly placed structures). |
| 1 | Poor visibility. Many blind spots. Inadequate lighting. No views or views blocked. Heavy visual clutter or disorienting spatial arrangement. |

#### Criterion 8: Opportunities to Talk and Listen

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Ambient noise consistently < 55 dB. Multiple conversational nooks and alcoves. Seating at conversational distances (0.5 - 3.0m). Pleasant background sounds (water, rustling leaves). No intrusive mechanical noise. |
| 4 | Ambient noise 55 - 60 dB. Some sheltered conversation areas. Most seating supports conversation. Background sounds mostly pleasant. |
| 3 | Ambient noise 60 - 65 dB. Conversation possible at normal voice in most areas. Some noisy zones near traffic. Seating arrangements partially support conversation. |
| 2 | Ambient noise 65 - 70 dB. Must raise voice to converse. Few sheltered spots. Seating arranged for individuals, not groups. |
| 1 | Ambient noise > 70 dB. Conversation very difficult. No acoustic shelter. Uncomfortable acoustic environment throughout. |

#### Criterion 9: Opportunities for Play, Exercise, and Activity

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Diverse activities for all ages: children's play (0 - 5 and 6 - 12), teens, adults, elderly. Formal (equipment, courts, fitness) and informal (open lawn, moveable elements, varied terrain). Water play, nature play, creative play. Accessible play. Seasonal activities accommodated. |
| 4 | Good range of activities for 3+ age groups. Mix of formal and informal. Some variety in play types. Generally accessible. |
| 3 | Moderate activity options for 2 - 3 age groups. Primarily formal (standard equipment) or primarily informal (open lawn). Limited variety. Basic accessibility. |
| 2 | Limited to 1 - 2 age groups. Only standard play equipment or only open lawn. Little variety. Accessibility barriers. |
| 1 | No activity opportunities. Nothing to do, climb, play with, or exercise on. Space discourages physical engagement. |

### ENJOYMENT

#### Criterion 10: Human Scale

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Perfectly proportioned: building enclosure 1/3 - 1x space width. Trees at human scale (8 - 15m). Rich ground-plane detail. Canopies, pergolas, arcades at 2.5 - 4m creating intimate overhead. No oversized blank facades. Every surface detailed at eye level. |
| 4 | Good proportions with minor exceptions. Trees and intermediate-scale elements present. Good ground-plane detail. Mostly intimate and comfortable. |
| 3 | Acceptable proportions but some areas feel oversized or underscaled. Some trees and human-scale elements. Moderate ground-plane detail. |
| 2 | Poor proportions in places: oversized buildings dominating, or large featureless areas. Limited tree cover. Sparse ground-plane detail. Feels somewhat impersonal. |
| 1 | Crushing scale: tall buildings, vast open areas, no trees or intermediate-scale elements. Featureless ground plane. Feels hostile, empty, or overwhelming. |

#### Criterion 11: Opportunities to Enjoy Climate

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Sun available in cold seasons (south-facing areas protected from building shadow). Deep shade in hot seasons. Wind-sheltered sun traps. Rain-covered areas (>10% of total). Seasonal planting. Space works beautifully in the "worst" season. |
| 4 | Good sun/shade balance with some seasonal gaps. Adequate wind shelter. Some rain cover. Works well in 3 of 4 seasons. |
| 3 | Moderate sun/shade options. Some wind exposure issues. Limited rain cover. Comfortable in 2 seasons, challenging in others. |
| 2 | Poor sun/shade balance (permanently shaded or fully exposed). Significant wind problems. No rain cover. Only comfortable in ideal weather. |
| 1 | No climate consideration. Permanently in shadow, or fully sun-exposed with no shade. Severe wind. No shelter. Unusable in bad weather. |

#### Criterion 12: Positive Sensory Experiences

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 | Beautiful and sensorially rich. Integrated art, water features, diverse planting with seasonal interest. High-quality natural materials (stone, timber, metal) that age gracefully. Framed views and terminated vistas. Fragrant planting. Sound of water. Impeccable maintenance. People take photos and share the space. |
| 4 | Attractive with good material quality. Some art or water. Good planting. Pleasant views. Well-maintained. |
| 3 | Acceptable aesthetics. Standard materials. Some planting. Maintenance adequate. Neither ugly nor beautiful. |
| 2 | Below average aesthetics. Cheap or inappropriate materials. Sparse planting. Visible maintenance neglect. |
| 1 | Ugly, neglected, or hostile. Poor materials in bad condition. No planting or dead planting. No aesthetic consideration. Litter, graffiti, damage visible. |

## 2. Application Guide by Space Type

The 12 criteria apply universally, but their relative importance shifts depending on the space type. Use this guide to weight your evaluation.

### Civic Plaza
- **Critical criteria (weight 1.5x):** 1 (traffic safety for events), 6 (seating), 10 (scale), 12 (aesthetic quality)
- **Important criteria (weight 1.0x):** 2, 3, 4, 5, 7, 8, 11
- **Lower priority (weight 0.75x):** 9 (play/exercise - less expected in a civic plaza)

### Neighborhood Park
- **Critical criteria (weight 1.5x):** 9 (play/exercise), 6 (seating), 11 (climate), 4 (walking)
- **Important criteria (weight 1.0x):** 1, 2, 3, 5, 7, 8, 10, 12
- All criteria are relevant in a neighborhood park; it must perform well across the board.

### Pocket Park
- **Critical criteria (weight 1.5x):** 6 (seating - the primary function), 3 (sensory protection - small space amplifies discomfort), 12 (aesthetic quality - small space demands beauty)
- **Important criteria (weight 1.0x):** 2, 5, 7, 8, 10, 11
- **Lower priority (weight 0.75x):** 1 (usually interior to a block), 4 (limited walking needed), 9 (limited space for activity)

### Linear Park / Greenway
- **Critical criteria (weight 1.5x):** 4 (walking - the primary function), 1 (traffic safety at crossings), 7 (seeing - sightlines along the corridor)
- **Important criteria (weight 1.0x):** 2, 3, 6, 9, 11, 12
- **Lower priority (weight 0.75x):** 5 (lingering less relevant in a movement corridor), 8 (talking less relevant while walking), 10 (scale varies along a long corridor)

### Waterfront Promenade
- **Critical criteria (weight 1.5x):** 7 (seeing - views to water), 6 (seating - with water views), 12 (aesthetic quality), 3 (wind protection - waterfronts are windy)
- **Important criteria (weight 1.0x):** 1, 2, 4, 5, 8, 10, 11
- **Lower priority (weight 0.75x):** 9 (limited space for activity on a promenade)

### Playground
- **Critical criteria (weight 1.5x):** 9 (play - the primary function), 1 (traffic safety - children are the users), 2 (crime safety - children), 6 (seating - for parents/caregivers)
- **Important criteria (weight 1.0x):** 3, 4, 7, 10, 11
- **Lower priority (weight 0.75x):** 5, 8, 12 (still relevant but secondary to safety and play)

### Community Garden
- **Critical criteria (weight 1.5x):** 2 (crime safety - vandalism/theft is a primary concern), 11 (climate - sun exposure for growing), 8 (talking - social function)
- **Important criteria (weight 1.0x):** 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, 12
- **Lower priority (weight 0.75x):** 1, 7, 10 (typically interior to a block, less scale concern)

### Streetscape / Green Street
- **Critical criteria (weight 1.5x):** 1 (traffic safety - shared space with vehicles), 4 (walking - primary function), 3 (sensory protection from traffic)
- **Important criteria (weight 1.0x):** 2, 5, 6, 7, 10, 11, 12
- **Lower priority (weight 0.75x):** 8, 9 (conversation and activity less central to a streetscape)

## 3. Observation Methodology (Gehl Institute Public Life Tools)

Conduct public life studies using the following standardized methods to understand how a space is currently used and to evaluate the success of a design after construction.

### 3.1 People Counting

**Purpose:** Measure pedestrian flows through and within the space.

**Method:**
1. Select counting gates: entry points to the space, key path intersections, threshold points between zones
2. Count all pedestrians passing each gate in both directions for a 10-minute interval every hour
3. Record: date, time, weather, gate location, count (in/out or total)
4. Standard survey schedule: weekday (Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday) + weekend day (Saturday), 8 AM to 10 PM, every hour

**Data Recording:**
| Date | Time | Weather | Gate | Direction | Count (10 min) | Projected/hr |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [date] | [time] | [temp, cloud, wind] | [gate ID] | [in/out/total] | [number] | [count x 6] |

### 3.2 People Tracing (Pedestrian Movement Mapping)

**Purpose:** Understand movement patterns, desire lines, and space utilization.

**Method:**
1. Obtain a scaled plan of the space
2. From a fixed observation point (elevated if possible), trace the path of every person entering the space for a 10-minute interval
3. Use different colors for different movement types (walking through, walking to destination, jogging, cycling)
4. Overlay all traces to reveal desire lines, dead zones, and conflict points
5. Standard schedule: 2 - 3 time periods (morning, midday, evening) on weekday + weekend

**Output:** A trace map showing movement density. Areas with many overlapping traces are high-use corridors. Areas with no traces are dead zones.

### 3.3 Stationary Activity Mapping (Staying Mapping)

**Purpose:** Understand where people choose to sit, stand, and linger - and what they do there.

**Method:**
1. Obtain a scaled plan of the space
2. Walk through the entire space and mark the location of every stationary person
3. Record the activity for each person using standard symbols:
   - Sitting on bench (solid circle)
   - Sitting on secondary seating (open circle)
   - Sitting on ground/grass (circle with dot)
   - Standing (triangle)
   - Lying down (rectangle)
   - Playing (star)
   - Exercising (X)
   - Commercial activity - vendor, performer (diamond)
4. Record gender (M/F) and age group (child/adult/elderly) where observable
5. Standard schedule: same as people counting (hourly snapshots)

**Output:** A stationary activity map showing where people choose to be. Clusters indicate successful sub-spaces. Empty areas indicate design problems.

### 3.4 Age and Gender Tally

**Purpose:** Understand who uses the space and whether it serves diverse populations.

**Method:**
1. During people counting intervals, also record estimated age group and gender:
   - Children (0 - 12)
   - Teenagers (13 - 17)
   - Young adults (18 - 30)
   - Adults (31 - 64)
   - Elderly (65+)
   - Male / Female / Unknown
2. Calculate percentages and compare to neighborhood demographics
3. Under-represented groups indicate design barriers or programmatic gaps

### 3.5 Walking Speed and Behavior

**Purpose:** Understand comfort and engagement along paths.

**Method:**
1. Measure a fixed distance along a path (20 - 50m)
2. Time individual pedestrians traversing the distance (stopwatch or video)
3. Record behavior: walking straight through, pausing, stopping to look, turning around, sitting down
4. Average walking speed: 4.5 - 5.0 km/h (normal), < 3.5 km/h (strolling/engaged), > 5.5 km/h (rushing/uncomfortable)
5. Slow walking speed indicates a comfortable, engaging space. Fast speed indicates discomfort or lack of interest.

### Template D: Space Quality Audit Checklist

Use this for a rapid field assessment of an existing or proposed public space.

```
PROJECT: ___________________________
DATE: _______________
OBSERVER: __________________________

SEATING
[ ] Benches present at minimum standard (1 per 30m path / 1 per 10m2)
[ ] Benches have backs and armrests (at least 50%)
[ ] Secondary seating available (walls, steps, planters)
[ ] Seating in both sun and shade
[ ] Seating faces activity or views (not blank walls)
[ ] Social seating (groups) and private seating (individual) available

PATHS
[ ] Primary paths follow desire lines
[ ] Path width >= 1.8m (main routes >= 2.5m)
[ ] Surface is firm, non-slip, well-drained
[ ] Universal access: no steps on main routes, max 5% gradient
[ ] Tactile indicators at crossings and hazards

LIGHTING
[ ] Paths lit to 10-20 lux
[ ] Gathering areas lit to 30-50 lux
[ ] Even distribution (no dark pockets)
[ ] Appropriate color temperature (2700-4000K)
[ ] Feature lighting on art, water, specimens

PLANTING
[ ] Canopy cover meets target (30-40% paved, 60-80% green)
[ ] Seasonal interest (year-round attractiveness)
[ ] Surveillance zone clear (no dense shrubs 0.6-2.0m)
[ ] Healthy, well-maintained plants

EDGES
[ ] Active edges on at least 2 sides (doors, windows, cafes)
[ ] No blank walls facing primary gathering areas
[ ] Edge seating and lingering supports present

WATER
[ ] Drinking water within 100m
[ ] Decorative water feature (if applicable): visible 50m, audible 20m

FACILITIES
[ ] Restrooms within 200m
[ ] Food/drink available within 100m
[ ] Waste bins every 30-50m
[ ] Bike parking available

TOTAL CHECKS PASSED: _____ / 25
```
