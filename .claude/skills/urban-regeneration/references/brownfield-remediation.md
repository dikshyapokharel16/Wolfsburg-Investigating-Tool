# Brownfield Remediation Reference

## Regulatory Framework

### Risk-Based Approach (International Standard)

Modern contaminated land regulation follows a source-pathway-receptor model:

```
SOURCE (contamination) → PATHWAY (migration route) → RECEPTOR (people, water, ecology)

All three must be present for a risk to exist.
Break any link in the chain → risk is managed.
```

**Risk assessment tiers:**

| Tier | Method | Cost | Accuracy | When Used |
|------|--------|------|----------|-----------|
| Tier 1 | Screening: compare soil/water concentrations to generic guidelines | Low ($5-15K) | Conservative (may overestimate risk) | Initial assessment; most sites |
| Tier 2 | Site-specific: adjust guideline values for actual site conditions | Medium ($15-40K) | Moderate | When Tier 1 flags exceedances |
| Tier 3 | Detailed quantitative: probabilistic modeling, Monte Carlo analysis | High ($40-100K+) | High (site-specific) | Complex sites, high-value land |

## Remediation Technology Selection

### Decision Framework

```
START: What type of contamination?

  Petroleum hydrocarbons (fuel, oil, diesel)
  |   → Soil: bioremediation, soil vapor extraction, thermal desorption
  |   → Groundwater: pump and treat, air sparging, monitored natural attenuation
  |
  Chlorinated solvents (TCE, PCE, dry cleaning)
  |   → Soil: chemical oxidation, thermal treatment
  |   → Groundwater: permeable reactive barrier (zero-valent iron), enhanced reductive dechlorination
  |
  Heavy metals (lead, arsenic, cadmium, chromium)
  |   → Soil: solidification/stabilization, soil washing, phytoremediation, dig and dump
  |   → Groundwater: pump and treat with precipitation, permeable reactive barrier
  |
  PAHs (coal tar, gas works, creosote)
  |   → Soil: bioremediation (slow), thermal desorption, dig and dump
  |   → Groundwater: pump and treat, bioremediation
  |
  Asbestos
  |   → Encapsulation in-situ, or careful removal by licensed contractor
  |   → Always specialist; never disturb without survey
  |
  Mixed contamination
  |   → Usually requires combination of techniques
  |   → Treatment train: physical separation → chemical treatment → biological polishing
```

### Technology Comparison

| Technology | Contaminants | Soil Type | Depth | Duration | Cost/m3 | Disruption |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dig and dump | All (soil) | All | 0-5m typical | Weeks | $80-300 | Very high |
| Bioremediation (in-situ) | Petroleum, PAHs | Permeable | 0-10m | 6-36 months | $20-80 | Low |
| Soil vapor extraction | VOCs | Permeable | 0-10m | 6-24 months | $30-100 | Low |
| Chemical oxidation (ISCO) | Chlorinated solvents, fuels | Variable | 0-20m | 3-12 months | $50-200 | Medium |
| Thermal desorption (ex-situ) | Petroleum, PAHs, SVOCs | All | Excavated | 1-6 months | $60-250 | High |
| Soil washing | Metals, fuels in sandy soils | Sand/gravel | Excavated | 1-3 months | $50-200 | High |
| Solidification/stabilization | Metals | All | 0-5m | Weeks-months | $40-150 | Medium |
| Phytoremediation | Metals (low levels) | Topsoil | 0-1m | 3-20 years | $5-30 | Very low |
| Permeable reactive barrier | Chlorinated solvents, metals (GW) | Permeable aquifer | 2-15m | Decades (passive) | $200-800/m2 | Medium (install) |

## Investigation Methods

### Sampling Strategy

**Grid-based sampling:**
- Regular grid: 10-25m spacing for initial screening
- Judgment sampling: targeted at identified hotspots
- Statistical: random within grid cells for representative data

**Minimum sampling density:**
| Site Area | Minimum Boreholes/Trial Pits |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| < 0.5 ha | 5-10 |
| 0.5-2 ha | 10-25 |
| 2-5 ha | 20-50 |
| 5-10 ha | 30-80 |
| > 10 ha | 5-10 per hectare |

**Sampling depth:**
- Minimum 1m below deepest suspected contamination
- At least to groundwater level (if within 5m)
- Include samples from each distinct soil layer

### Monitoring Requirements

**During remediation:**
- Dust monitoring (PM10) at site boundary: daily during excavation
- Noise monitoring: at nearest sensitive receptor
- Groundwater levels and quality: monthly
- VOC monitoring: continuous in excavation areas
- Odor assessment: daily at site boundary

**Post-remediation validation:**
- Soil validation sampling: 1 sample per 25m3 of treated soil minimum
- Groundwater monitoring: quarterly for 2-4 years post-completion
- Gas monitoring: 6-12 months of monitoring if methane/CO2 risk
- Verification report: independent review of all data

## Brownfield Redevelopment Incentives

### Common Government Incentives

| Incentive | Mechanism | Typical Value |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| Tax increment financing (TIF) | Future property tax uplift funds current remediation | 50-100% of remediation cost |
| Brownfield tax credit | Tax credit for remediation expenditure | 15-25% of qualifying costs |
| Environmental insurance | Government-backed insurance against unknown contamination | Reduces contingency by 5-15% |
| Reduced planning obligations | Lower affordable housing or infrastructure contributions | 5-15% of development cost |
| Gap funding grant | Direct grant to bridge feasibility gap | $500K-10M+ |
| Accelerated depreciation | Faster depreciation of remediation expenditure | Tax benefit |
| Enterprise zone | Reduced taxes in designated regeneration areas | Property tax reduction |
| Compulsory purchase | Government acquires contaminated land at written-down value | Enables assembly |
| Simplified planning zone | Pre-approved planning consent for specified development | Reduced time and risk |
| Liability transfer | Government assumes long-term contamination liability | Reduces developer risk |

## Gas Protection Measures

For sites with ground gas risk (landfill, contaminated ground, coal measures):

### Gas Characterization

| Gas | Source | Risk | Action Level |
|-----|--------|------|-------------|
| Methane (CH4) | Biodegradation, landfill, coal | Explosion (5-15% in air) | > 1% v/v |
| Carbon dioxide (CO2) | Biodegradation, landfill | Asphyxiation (> 5%) | > 5% v/v |
| Hydrogen sulfide (H2S) | Anaerobic conditions | Toxic (> 10 ppm) | > 5 ppm |
| Carbon monoxide (CO) | Incomplete combustion | Toxic (> 50 ppm) | > 30 ppm |
| Radon (Rn) | Geological | Lung cancer | > 200 Bq/m3 |
| VOCs (various) | Contaminated ground | Toxic | Compound-specific |

### Gas Protection Specification

| Characteristic | CS1 (Very Low) | CS2 (Low) | CS3 (Moderate) | CS4 (High) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Gas screening value | < 0.1 L/hr | 0.1-0.7 L/hr | 0.7-3.5 L/hr | > 3.5 L/hr |
| Protection required | Basic DPM | DPM + ventilation | Reinforced membrane + ventilation + monitoring | Full gas-resistant design |
| Membrane | Standard DPM | Proprietary 0.5mm | Reinforced 1.2mm, independently verified | Multi-layer, positive pressure |
| Ventilation | Not required | Passive sub-floor | Passive or active sub-floor | Active with alarm |
| Monitoring | Not required | Not required | Consider | Required |
| Cost premium | $0-5/m2 | $10-20/m2 | $30-60/m2 | $80-200/m2 |

DPM = Damp-proof membrane
