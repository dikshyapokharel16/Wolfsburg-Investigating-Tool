## Internal Capture Rates

When multiple land uses share a site, some trips are "internal" -- they occur
between uses on the same site and do not impact external roads.

### Internal Capture by Mix Quality

| Mix Quality | Internal Capture Rate | Example |
|-------------|----------------------|---------|
| Single use (residential only) | 0-5% | Housing estate |
| Dual use (residential + retail) | 8-15% | Apartment building with ground-floor shops |
| Multi-use (3+ uses, vertical mix) | 15-25% | Mixed-use tower with office, retail, residential |
| Integrated district (walk-connected) | 20-30% | Master-planned mixed-use neighborhood |
| Compact urban center (fine-grained) | 25-40% | Traditional city center, European old town |

### Internal Capture by Trip Purpose

| Trip Purpose | Typical Internal Capture (Mixed-Use District) |
|---|---|
| Home-to-work | 15-25% (if jobs-housing balance exists) |
| Home-to-school | 30-50% (if school is on-site) |
| Home-to-shop | 20-40% (if retail is on-site) |
| Work-to-lunch | 60-80% (if F&B is on-site) |
| Work-to-errands | 15-30% (if services are on-site) |

## Growth Factors

For long-term transport planning, apply background growth to baseline traffic:

| Horizon | Annual Growth (Developed) | Annual Growth (Developing) |
|---------|--------------------------|----------------------------|
| 0-5 years | 0.5-1.5% | 2.0-5.0% |
| 5-15 years | 0.5-1.0% | 1.5-3.5% |
| 15-30 years | 0.0-0.5% | 1.0-2.5% |

**Note:** Growth factors should be reduced when:
- Road pricing is implemented (reduce by 10-20%)
- High-quality transit is introduced (reduce by 15-30%)
- Remote work trends continue (reduce by 5-15%)
- Autonomous vehicles emerge (effect uncertain; plan for 0-10% increase in
  vehicle trips due to induced demand from empty repositioning trips)
