# BREEAM Communities: Complete Credit Reference

Comprehensive reference for BREEAM Communities (Building Research Establishment Environmental Assessment Method), the leading sustainability assessment framework for neighborhood and community-scale developments in the United Kingdom and Europe. This document covers all five assessment categories with their credits, weightings, and assessment criteria.

---

## Overview

BREEAM Communities is developed and administered by the Building Research Establishment (BRE) in the United Kingdom. It assesses the sustainability of large-scale development proposals at the masterplanning and infrastructure design stages. The assessment is carried out by a licensed BREEAM Communities assessor at three stages:

1. **Step 1**: Establishing the principles of the development (site context, community engagement, vision)
2. **Step 2**: Determining the layout of the development (masterplan, infrastructure, movement)
3. **Step 3**: Designing the details (building specifications, landscape, implementation)

### Scoring and Certification

BREEAM Communities uses a weighted percentage score across five categories:

| Rating | Score Required |
|--------|---------------|
| Pass | 30% |
| Good | 45% |
| Very Good | 55% |
| Excellent | 70% |
| Outstanding | 85% |

Each category is weighted to reflect its relative importance, and the final score is the sum of weighted category scores.

### Category Weightings

| Category | Code | Weighting | Focus |
|----------|------|-----------|-------|
| Governance | GO | 9.3% | Leadership, consultation, community engagement, management |
| Social and Economic Well-being | SE | 42.3% | Housing, services, economy, inclusivity, culture |
| Resources and Energy | RE | 21.6% | Energy strategy, water, materials, waste, resource efficiency |
| Land Use and Ecology | LE | 12.6% | Site selection, ecology, landscape, biodiversity |
| Transport and Movement | TM | 14.2% | Connectivity, transit, walking, cycling, parking |
| **TOTAL** | | **100%** | |

## Category 1: Governance (GO) -- 9.3% Weighting

Governance credits assess the processes, structures, and commitments that underpin sustainable development decision-making.

### GO 01: Consultation Plan

**Assessment Stage**: Step 1
**Credits Available**: 3
**Intent**: Ensure meaningful community engagement throughout the development process.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A consultation plan has been prepared identifying stakeholders, consultation methods, and timeline. Key stakeholders include: local residents, community groups, local authority, statutory consultees, hard-to-reach groups.
- **1 credit**: Evidence of pre-application engagement with the local community including at least 2 public events (exhibitions, workshops, drop-in sessions) held at accessible venues and times.
- **1 credit**: A feedback mechanism is in place demonstrating how consultation responses have influenced the design. A consultation report summarizing issues raised, responses given, and design changes made is available.

**Design Implications**: Begin community engagement early (before design freeze). Document all engagement activities. Show demonstrable design changes resulting from community input.

**Evidence**: Consultation plan, event records (attendance, materials, photographs), feedback logs, consultation report.

---

### GO 02: Consultation and Engagement

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2 and Step 3
**Credits Available**: 4
**Intent**: Continue community engagement through design development and into implementation.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: Ongoing engagement through the design development stage (Step 2), with at least 2 additional events or workshops focused on detailed design issues (layout, open space, transport, community facilities).
- **1 credit**: Engagement with hard-to-reach groups (youth, elderly, disabled, ethnic minorities, non-English speakers) through targeted outreach methods (translated materials, accessible venues, youth workshops).
- **1 credit**: A community governance structure is established for the post-completion phase (residents' association, community trust, management company) with defined roles and responsibilities.
- **1 credit**: A long-term community engagement strategy is in place covering the first 5 years of occupation, including community development programs, social integration activities, and feedback mechanisms.

**Evidence**: Engagement records, translated materials, community governance documents, long-term strategy.

---

### GO 03: Design Review

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2
**Credits Available**: 2
**Intent**: Ensure design quality through independent professional review.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: The masterplan has been reviewed by an independent design review panel (e.g., CABE Design Review, local authority design panel, or equivalent).
- **1 credit**: The design team has responded to the review panel's recommendations with documented design changes or justified reasons for not adopting recommendations.

**Evidence**: Design review panel report, design team response, evidence of design changes.

---

### GO 04: Community Management of Facilities

**Assessment Stage**: Step 3
**Credits Available**: 2
**Intent**: Ensure long-term management and maintenance of shared facilities and spaces.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A management plan for communal facilities (open spaces, community buildings, play areas, allotments, sustainable drainage systems) is in place with identified responsible party and funding mechanism.
- **1 credit**: The management plan includes provisions for community involvement in decision-making about facility management, budgets, and programming.

**Evidence**: Management plan, maintenance schedules, funding mechanism, community governance structure.

## Category 2: Social and Economic Well-being (SE) -- 42.3% Weighting

This is the most heavily weighted category, reflecting the importance of social outcomes in sustainable communities.

### SE 01: Economic Impact

**Assessment Stage**: Step 1
**Credits Available**: 3
**Intent**: Ensure the development makes a positive contribution to the local economy.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: An economic impact assessment has been prepared, quantifying the development's impact on local employment, business rates, consumer spending, and supply chain activity during construction and occupation.
- **1 credit**: The development includes provisions for local employment during construction (e.g., local labor agreements, apprenticeship programs, supply chain commitments).
- **1 credit**: The development includes long-term economic sustainability measures: space for local businesses, affordable commercial space, business support programs, or enterprise facilities.

---

### SE 02: Demographic Needs and Priorities

**Assessment Stage**: Step 1
**Credits Available**: 3
**Intent**: Ensure the development responds to the demographic profile and needs of the local area.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A demographic needs assessment has been prepared, analyzing the local population profile (age, household size, income, ethnicity, disability) and identifying housing and service needs.
- **1 credit**: The development's housing mix (type, size, tenure, affordability) is informed by the demographic needs assessment and local housing needs assessment.
- **1 credit**: The development includes facilities and services identified as priorities by the demographic needs assessment (e.g., childcare, healthcare, elderly services, youth facilities, places of worship).

---

### SE 03: Flood Risk

**Assessment Stage**: Step 1 and Step 2
**Credits Available**: 3
**Intent**: Ensure the development does not increase flood risk and is resilient to flooding.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A flood risk assessment (FRA) has been prepared in accordance with national planning policy (e.g., NPPF in England, TAN 15 in Wales).
- **1 credit**: The development is located in Flood Zone 1 (low probability) or, if in a higher flood zone, passes the sequential test and exception test as required by national policy.
- **1 credit**: Sustainable drainage systems (SuDS) are incorporated to manage surface water runoff, with a target of no increase in runoff rates compared to the greenfield (or pre-development) condition for events up to the 1-in-100-year storm plus climate change allowance.

---

### SE 04: Noise

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2 and Step 3
**Credits Available**: 3
**Intent**: Protect residents and occupants from excessive noise and ensure acceptable acoustic conditions.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A noise impact assessment has been prepared, identifying noise sources (road traffic, rail, aircraft, industrial, commercial) and predicting noise levels at the development.
- **1 credit**: The masterplan layout is designed to mitigate noise impacts through spatial planning measures: noise-sensitive uses (residential, schools, healthcare) located away from noise sources; non-sensitive uses (commercial, parking structures) used as noise barriers; building orientation and layout minimize noise exposure.
- **1 credit**: Where noise levels exceed guideline values (WHO, BS 8233, or equivalent), mitigation measures are incorporated in building design (enhanced glazing, acoustic ventilation, sound insulation) to achieve acceptable internal noise levels.

---

### SE 05: Housing Provision

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2
**Credits Available**: 4
**Intent**: Provide a range of housing types and tenures to meet identified needs.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A mix of housing types (detached, semi-detached, terraced, apartment) is provided, with no single type comprising more than 60% of total units.
- **1 credit**: A mix of housing sizes (1-bed, 2-bed, 3-bed, 4+ bed) is provided, responsive to the local housing needs assessment.
- **1 credit**: Affordable housing is provided at or above the local planning authority's requirement (typically 20-40% of total units in the UK), with tenure mix (social rent, shared ownership, affordable rent) responsive to local needs.
- **1 credit**: Dwellings are designed to meet accessible and adaptable standards (Building Regulations Part M4(2) Category 2, or equivalent) for a minimum of 10% of units, with 2%+ meeting Part M4(3) wheelchair-accessible standards.

---

### SE 06: Delivery of Services, Facilities and Amenities

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2 and Step 3
**Credits Available**: 4
**Intent**: Ensure residents have access to essential services and community facilities.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A community facilities and services strategy is prepared, identifying what facilities are needed, when they will be delivered (phasing), and how they will be funded and managed.
- **1 credit**: Essential services (primary school, healthcare, local retail, public open space) are within 800m walking distance of 90%+ of dwellings.
- **1 credit**: Community facilities (community hall, sports/leisure, library, place of worship) are within 800m walking distance of 90%+ of dwellings, or a plan for their phased delivery is in place.
- **1 credit**: Facilities are delivered early in the development phasing (concurrent with or ahead of residential occupation), not deferred to later phases.

---

### SE 07: Public Realm

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2 and Step 3
**Credits Available**: 4
**Intent**: Create a high-quality, inclusive, and safe public realm.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A public realm strategy is prepared addressing: hierarchy of spaces (civic, neighborhood, local), character and identity, materials and planting, lighting, street furniture, public art, wayfinding.
- **1 credit**: The public realm is designed for inclusivity: accessible routes (DDA compliant), seating at regular intervals (50-100m), tactile paving at crossings, adequate lighting for safety and comfort (minimum 10 lux on primary routes), varied play opportunities (natural play, equipped play, teen hangout areas).
- **1 credit**: The public realm incorporates measures for personal safety: natural surveillance (windows and doors overlooking public spaces), clear sight lines, defensible space hierarchy (public-semi-public-semi-private-private), active frontages on primary routes, avoidance of underpasses, blind corners, and hidden areas.
- **1 credit**: Maintenance and management plan for the public realm is in place with identified responsible party, standards, and funding mechanism for at least 10 years.

---

### SE 08: Microclimate

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2
**Credits Available**: 3
**Intent**: Ensure comfortable outdoor microclimate conditions for pedestrians and public space users.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A microclimate assessment has been prepared, analyzing wind conditions (pedestrian-level wind comfort and safety using Lawson criteria or equivalent), solar access, and thermal comfort in key public spaces.
- **1 credit**: The masterplan layout is designed to optimize microclimate: buildings oriented and spaced to provide solar access in winter and shade in summer; wind mitigation through building placement, landscaping, and screens; south-facing public spaces (northern hemisphere) for solar amenity.
- **1 credit**: Detailed design of key public spaces incorporates microclimate mitigation measures: tree canopy for shade, sheltered seating areas, wind screens or building podiums at base of tall buildings, water features for evaporative cooling.

---

### SE 09 - SE 12: Additional Social Credits

| Credit | Credits | Focus |
|--------|---------|-------|
| SE 09: Utilities | 2 | Adequate utility infrastructure (water, wastewater, electricity, gas, telecommunications) with capacity planning, resilience, and future-proofing |
| SE 10: Adapting to Climate Change | 3 | Climate change risk assessment, adaptation measures in masterplan (urban greening, water management, overheating prevention, resilient infrastructure) |
| SE 11: Green Infrastructure | 3 | Integrated green infrastructure strategy: urban tree canopy (30%+ coverage at maturity), green corridors, rain gardens, green roofs, biodiversity net gain |
| SE 12: Local Parking | 3 | Context-appropriate parking provision: parking standards based on accessibility audit (transit quality, walking/cycling provision), car club provision, EV charging infrastructure (20%+ active, 80%+ passive), cycle parking exceeding minimum standards |

## Category 3: Resources and Energy (RE) -- 21.6% Weighting

### RE 01: Energy Strategy

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2
**Credits Available**: 4
**Intent**: Establish an energy strategy that minimizes carbon emissions.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: An energy strategy has been prepared with a carbon reduction target for the development (minimum 20% reduction below Building Regulations baseline).
- **1 credit**: The energy strategy follows the energy hierarchy: (1) lean (reduce demand through fabric-first approach), (2) clean (efficient supply through district energy or heat networks), (3) green (renewable energy generation).
- **1 credit**: A feasibility study for district energy (heat network) has been completed, with implementation plan if viable (typically viable above 50 DU/ha or with anchor loads such as leisure center, hospital, school).
- **1 credit**: On-site renewable energy generation is included (solar PV, wind, biomass, ground-source heat pumps) meeting at least 10% of the development's regulated energy demand.

---

### RE 02: Existing Buildings and Infrastructure

**Assessment Stage**: Step 1 and Step 2
**Credits Available**: 2
**Intent**: Maximize the reuse of existing buildings and infrastructure.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: An audit of existing buildings and infrastructure on the site has been completed, assessing condition, heritage value, and potential for reuse/adaptation.
- **1 credit**: Where viable, existing buildings and infrastructure are retained and adapted rather than demolished. A minimum of 50% of existing building floor area (by area) is retained, or a justified case for demolition is provided with materials recovery plan.

---

### RE 03: Water Strategy

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2 and Step 3
**Credits Available**: 3
**Intent**: Minimize potable water consumption.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A water strategy is prepared with targets for indoor water consumption (max 110 liters/person/day, or 105 l/p/d for higher credit) and outdoor water use (50%+ reduction in irrigation demand through species selection and efficient irrigation).
- **1 credit**: Rainwater harvesting and/or greywater recycling systems are incorporated for non-potable uses (toilet flushing, irrigation, car washing).
- **1 credit**: Smart water metering is provided to all dwellings and commercial units, with leak detection and consumption feedback to occupants.

---

### RE 04: Sustainable Buildings

**Assessment Stage**: Step 3
**Credits Available**: 4
**Intent**: Ensure individual buildings achieve high environmental performance.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A minimum of 50% of residential buildings (by floor area) are designed to achieve BREEAM Home Quality Mark "4 Star" or equivalent.
- **1 credit**: A minimum of 50% of non-residential buildings (by floor area) are designed to achieve BREEAM New Construction "Very Good" or equivalent.
- **1 credit**: Enhanced targets: 80%+ of residential at HQM "4 Star" or 50%+ at "5 Star".
- **1 credit**: Enhanced targets: 80%+ of non-residential at BREEAM "Very Good" or 50%+ at "Excellent".

---

### RE 05: Resource Efficiency

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2 and Step 3
**Credits Available**: 3
**Intent**: Promote efficient use of materials and minimize waste.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A site waste management plan (SWMP) is prepared for the construction phase with a target to divert 90%+ of construction waste from landfill.
- **1 credit**: A materials strategy is prepared prioritizing: responsibly sourced materials (FSC timber, BES 6001 certified), recycled content (30%+ by value of key materials), low-embodied-carbon materials (concrete with GGBS or PFA, timber structure, recycled aggregates).
- **1 credit**: Operational waste facilities are designed into the development: segregated waste collection (recyclables, compostables, residual) within 30m of every dwelling entrance, community composting facilities, bulky waste storage and collection.

---

### RE 06: Transport Carbon Emissions

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2
**Credits Available**: 3
**Intent**: Minimize carbon emissions from transport associated with the development.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A transport carbon assessment is prepared, estimating per-capita transport CO2 emissions for the development and comparing to local/national benchmarks.
- **1 credit**: Transport emissions are reduced by 10%+ below benchmark through: location (proximity to employment and services), transit provision, walking/cycling infrastructure, parking restrictions, and travel plan measures.
- **1 credit**: Transport emissions are reduced by 20%+ below benchmark.

---

## Category 4: Land Use and Ecology (LE) -- 12.6% Weighting

### LE 01: Ecology Strategy

**Assessment Stage**: Step 1 and Step 2
**Credits Available**: 3
**Intent**: Protect and enhance ecological value.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: An ecological assessment (Phase 1 Habitat Survey at minimum) has been completed, identifying habitats, species, and ecological value of the site.
- **1 credit**: The development achieves a minimum 10% biodiversity net gain (BNG) using the DEFRA Biodiversity Metric or equivalent, through on-site habitat creation, enhancement, and management.
- **1 credit**: Enhanced: 20%+ biodiversity net gain, or achievement of exemplary ecological design (creation of priority habitats, species-specific design measures, wildlife corridors connecting to off-site habitats).

---

### LE 02: Land Use

**Assessment Stage**: Step 1
**Credits Available**: 3
**Intent**: Prioritize development on previously developed land and minimize greenfield consumption.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: 75%+ of the site is previously developed land (brownfield).
- **1 credit**: 95%+ of the site is previously developed land.
- **1 credit**: Where greenfield land is developed, the land is of low ecological and agricultural value (not grade 1, 2, or 3a agricultural land).

---

### LE 03: Water Pollution

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2
**Credits Available**: 2
**Intent**: Prevent pollution of water courses from surface water runoff.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: Surface water drainage strategy includes treatment train (source control, conveyance, treatment, discharge) to achieve appropriate pollution mitigation for the site's pollution hazard level.
- **1 credit**: SuDS treatment train achieves two or more treatment stages before discharge to any water course (e.g., permeable pavement + bioswale, or green roof + rain garden + detention pond).

---

### LE 04: Landscape

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2 and Step 3
**Credits Available**: 3
**Intent**: Create high-quality, functional, and sustainable landscape.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A landscape strategy is prepared by a qualified landscape architect, addressing: character and identity, views and vistas, topography, soil management, planting design (native and climate-resilient species), hard landscape materials, boundary treatments.
- **1 credit**: Tree canopy coverage target of 30%+ at maturity across the development (calculated using canopy spread of proposed tree species at 25-year growth).
- **1 credit**: Productive landscape is incorporated: allotments/community gardens (minimum 1 plot per 20 dwellings), orchard planting, edible landscaping in public spaces, rain gardens and bioswales as productive/educational landscapes.

---

### LE 05 - LE 06: Additional Land Use Credits

| Credit | Credits | Focus |
|--------|---------|-------|
| LE 05: Habitat | 2 | Creation of new habitats (wildflower meadows, wetlands, woodland), wildlife corridors, green bridges, nesting and roosting features integrated into buildings (swift bricks, bat boxes, hedgehog highways) |
| LE 06: Flood Risk Management | 2 | SuDS designed to manage surface water to greenfield runoff rates; multi-functional SuDS (biodiversity, amenity, water quality, flood storage); climate change allowance (+40% rainfall intensity) incorporated |

---

## Category 5: Transport and Movement (TM) -- 14.2% Weighting

### TM 01: Transport Assessment

**Assessment Stage**: Step 1 and Step 2
**Credits Available**: 3
**Intent**: Ensure transport impacts are assessed and mitigated.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A transport assessment (TA) has been prepared in accordance with national and local guidance, assessing: trip generation, mode split, traffic impact on surrounding network, parking demand, servicing and deliveries.
- **1 credit**: The transport assessment includes a target mode split with walking, cycling, and public transport accounting for 50%+ of all trips generated by the development.
- **1 credit**: A travel plan framework is prepared with: targets for mode split, measures to promote sustainable travel (cycle parking, cycle hire, car club, real-time transit information, travel information packs for new residents), monitoring and review mechanisms.

---

### TM 02: Safe and Appealing Streets

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2 and Step 3
**Credits Available**: 4
**Intent**: Design streets that prioritize pedestrians and cyclists and create safe, attractive environments.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: Street hierarchy is designed with a user hierarchy placing pedestrians first, then cyclists, then public transport, then private vehicles. Design speed on residential streets is 20 mph (30 km/h) or less.
- **1 credit**: All streets have continuous footways on both sides (minimum 2.0m clear width, 3.0m on primary routes), with dropped kerbs and tactile paving at all crossings.
- **1 credit**: Streets are designed according to Manual for Streets (MfS) and Manual for Streets 2 (MfS2) principles: buildings front onto streets, parking does not dominate the streetscene, on-street parking is integrated with landscape, streets are designed as places (not just movement corridors).
- **1 credit**: Traffic calming and speed reduction measures are designed into the street geometry (not relying on signs or enforcement): narrow carriageways, tight junction radii, raised crossings, shared surfaces, chicanes, and gateway features at entry points.

---

### TM 03: Cycling Facilities

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2 and Step 3
**Credits Available**: 3
**Intent**: Promote cycling through safe, convenient infrastructure and storage.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: A cycle network is provided connecting all parts of the development to the wider municipal cycle network, schools, employment, and local services. Routes are direct, safe, and well-lit.
- **1 credit**: Cycle storage is provided for every dwelling: minimum 1 secure, covered cycle parking space per bedroom (up to 2 per dwelling for houses, up to 1.5 per dwelling for apartments). Visitor cycle parking at community facilities, shops, and public spaces (minimum 2 stands per facility).
- **1 credit**: Enhanced cycling infrastructure: segregated cycle tracks on streets with design speeds above 20 mph (30 km/h) or traffic volumes above 5,000 AADT; cycle maintenance stations (pump, tools) at community facilities; cycle hire/sharing scheme.

---

### TM 04: Access to Public Transport

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2
**Credits Available**: 3
**Intent**: Ensure convenient access to quality public transport.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: 90%+ of dwellings are within 400m walking distance of a bus stop served by at least 2 routes with a combined frequency of 20+ services per day (Monday-Friday).
- **1 credit**: 50%+ of dwellings are within 800m walking distance of a rail station, tram stop, or BRT stop with 40+ services per day (Monday-Friday).
- **1 credit**: Public transport stops within the development include: shelter, seating, lighting, real-time passenger information displays, accessible boarding (raised kerbs, tactile paving, wheelchair space on buses), and are located on desire lines between homes and the network.

---

### TM 05: Parking Strategy

**Assessment Stage**: Step 2
**Credits Available**: 2
**Intent**: Ensure parking provision is appropriate to context and supports sustainable transport.

**Criteria**:
- **1 credit**: Parking provision is based on a context-sensitive assessment (not blanket standards) considering: public transport accessibility, walking and cycling provision, car ownership data, car club membership potential. Parking ratios are at or below 1.5 spaces per dwelling for suburban areas, at or below 1.0 for urban areas, and at or below 0.5 for highly accessible urban locations.
- **1 credit**: Electric vehicle charging infrastructure: minimum 20% of parking spaces with active EV charge points (7kW minimum), 80% of remaining spaces with passive provision (ducting and capacity for future installation). Car club bays provided (minimum 1 per 50 dwellings in urban areas).

## BREEAM Communities Assessment Process

### Stage 1: Pre-Assessment

- Appoint a licensed BREEAM Communities assessor
- Determine the project's scope and boundaries
- Conduct a pre-assessment to estimate achievable rating
- Identify prerequisites and key credits to target
- Estimated cost: assessor fees + BRE registration = GBP 15,000-40,000

### Stage 2: Full Assessment

Three-step assessment aligned with design stages:
- **Step 1** (Outline/Strategic Design): GO credits, SE context credits, LE site assessment, TM transport assessment
- **Step 2** (Detailed Design/Masterplan): SE design credits, RE strategy credits, LE ecology and landscape, TM infrastructure design
- **Step 3** (Technical/Implementation Design): RE building performance, SE detailed design quality, final scoring

### Stage 3: Certification

- Assessor submits evidence to BRE Quality Assurance
- BRE reviews, may request additional information
- Certificate issued upon satisfactory review
- Post-construction audit may be required for certain credits

### Key Differences from LEED-ND

| Feature | LEED-ND | BREEAM Communities |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| Geography | US-focused (globally applicable) | UK-focused (internationally adapted) |
| Scoring | Points (110 total) | Weighted percentage |
| Categories | 5 (SLL, NPD, GIB, ID, RP) | 5 (GO, SE, RE, LE, TM) |
| Social emphasis | Moderate (housing, community) | High (42.3% weighting on SE) |
| Process emphasis | Moderate (ID credits for process) | High (GO credits, 3-step assessment) |
| Building performance | Required (prerequisites for green buildings) | Referenced (credit for BREEAM building ratings) |
| Ecology | Moderate (conservation, restoration) | Strong (biodiversity net gain, DEFRA metric) |
| Climate adaptation | Limited (regional priority credits) | Strong (SE-10 dedicated credit) |
| Assessor | LEED AP ND (optional, 1 credit) | Licensed BREEAM Communities assessor (mandatory) |
