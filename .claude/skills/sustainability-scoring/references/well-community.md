# WELL Community Standard: Complete Reference

Comprehensive reference for the WELL Community Standard, developed by the International WELL Building Institute (IWBI). This is the leading standard for designing, implementing, and certifying health-promoting community-scale developments. The standard evaluates the built environment's impact on human health and well-being across ten core concepts plus an Innovation concept.

---

## Overview

The WELL Community Standard adapts the WELL Building Standard (v2) to the community and neighborhood scale. It addresses the public realm, outdoor spaces, infrastructure, and shared amenities that affect the health and well-being of all community members. The standard recognizes that health outcomes are shaped not just by individual buildings but by the design of streets, parks, transit systems, food environments, and social infrastructure.

### Structure

The standard is organized into 11 concepts, each containing:
- **Preconditions**: mandatory requirements that must be met for certification (equivalent to prerequisites)
- **Optimizations**: optional credits that earn points toward a higher score

### Certification Levels

| Level | Requirements |
|-------|-------------|
| **WELL Community** | Meet all preconditions + minimum number of optimizations per concept |
| **WELL Community - Silver** | Meet all preconditions + higher number of optimizations |
| **WELL Community - Gold** | Meet all preconditions + higher number of optimizations |
| **WELL Community - Platinum** | Meet all preconditions + highest number of optimizations |

The exact point thresholds for each level are determined by IWBI and published in the current version of the standard. Projects must achieve optimizations across multiple concepts (not concentrated in one or two areas) to demonstrate a holistic approach to health.

### Assessment Process

1. **Registration**: Register the project with IWBI
2. **Documentation Review**: Submit documentation (policies, plans, specifications) to IWBI for review
3. **Performance Verification**: On-site testing and verification by a WELL Performance Testing Agent (air quality measurements, water quality testing, light measurements, acoustic measurements)
4. **Certification**: IWBI issues certification upon successful review and verification
5. **Recertification**: Required every 3 years to maintain certification (ensures ongoing performance)

## Concept 1: Air (A)

**Intent**: Minimize human exposure to harmful air pollutants in outdoor and semi-outdoor environments within the community.

### Preconditions

**A01 Smoke-Free Environments** (Precondition)
- Prohibit smoking within 7.5m (25 ft) of all building entries, operable windows, air intakes, and outdoor dining areas
- Designate 100% of common outdoor spaces (parks, plazas, playgrounds, community gardens) as smoke-free
- Install signage at all smoke-free zones
- Provide smoking cessation resources through community health programming

**A02 Air Quality Monitoring** (Precondition)
- Install outdoor air quality monitoring stations within the community (minimum 1 per 20 ha or 1 per 2,000 residents, whichever is greater)
- Monitor: PM2.5, PM10, O3, NO2 at minimum; CO and SO2 recommended
- Display real-time air quality data on public digital signage and community mobile application
- Provide health advisories when AQI exceeds WHO guideline values

### Optimizations

**A03 Pollution Management**
- Implement a community-wide pollution reduction plan addressing: vehicle emissions (low-emission zones, EV infrastructure, anti-idling policies), construction dust (site management plans), landscape maintenance (no gas-powered leaf blowers, electric equipment), and industrial emissions (if applicable)
- Design streets and open spaces to minimize pollution exposure: locate pedestrian and cycling routes away from high-traffic roads; use vegetation barriers (dense hedge or tree planting) between high-traffic roads and pedestrian areas; orient building air intakes away from traffic

**A04 Construction Pollution Prevention**
- During construction: dust suppression (water spraying, covered loads, wheel washing), equipment emissions standards (Tier 4 / Stage V engines), monitoring of PM2.5 at site boundary (not to exceed 50 ug/m3 24-hour average), community notification of high-dust activities

**A05 Enhanced Air Quality**
- Achieve outdoor air quality meeting WHO Air Quality Guidelines (annual mean PM2.5 < 5 ug/m3, NO2 < 10 ug/m3) in 90%+ of the community area
- If existing conditions exceed guidelines: implement a documented air quality improvement plan with measurable targets and timelines

## Concept 2: Water (W)

**Intent**: Ensure access to safe, clean drinking water and promote responsible water management.

### Preconditions

**W01 Drinking Water Quality** (Precondition)
- All drinking water supplied to the community must meet WHO Drinking Water Quality Guidelines or national/local standards (whichever is more stringent)
- Annual water quality testing by accredited laboratory for: microbiological parameters (E. coli, total coliforms), chemical parameters (lead, copper, arsenic, nitrates, disinfection byproducts), physical parameters (turbidity, pH, taste, odor)
- Results published and accessible to all community members

**W02 Water Access** (Precondition)
- Provide free public drinking water fountains or refill stations at a maximum spacing of 200m in developed areas (streets, parks, public spaces)
- All fountains/stations must be: accessible (wheelchair height and approach), maintained (weekly cleaning, annual servicing), drinking-water quality (filtered if source quality is variable), operational year-round (freeze protection in cold climates)

### Optimizations

**W03 Water Quality Enhancement**
- Install point-of-entry or point-of-use water filtration at all residential buildings (activated carbon + sediment filter at minimum; reverse osmosis for areas with elevated TDS or specific contaminants)
- Test water quality at point of use (tap) annually (not just at source or treatment plant)

**W04 Water Conservation**
- Community-wide water consumption target: maximum 100 liters per person per day (indoor domestic use)
- Smart water metering for all dwellings and commercial units with leak detection alerts
- Rainwater harvesting for non-potable uses: minimum 50% of outdoor irrigation from rainwater or recycled water
- Greywater recycling for toilet flushing in community buildings (where permitted by local codes)
- Community dashboard showing real-time water consumption and conservation progress

**W05 Water Resilience**
- Emergency water supply plan: 72-hour potable water reserve (3 liters/person/day minimum) stored or available through emergency supply agreements
- Redundant water supply (dual source or on-site storage) for communities in water-stressed areas
- Flood resilience: SuDS managing 1-in-100-year storm + climate change allowance; no habitable space below design flood level

**W06 Stormwater Management**
- Manage stormwater quality through a treatment train: source control (green roofs, permeable pavement) + conveyance (swales, filter strips) + treatment (bioretention, constructed wetland) + discharge
- Target: 80%+ of annual rainfall volume treated through green infrastructure before any discharge to combined sewer or water course
- Stormwater features designed as amenities: visible, accessible, educational signage, integrated with landscape design

## Concept 3: Nourishment (N)

**Intent**: Promote healthy eating by increasing access to nutritious food, supporting food literacy, and creating food environments that encourage healthy choices.

### Preconditions

**N01 Healthy Food Access** (Precondition)
- 90%+ of dwellings within 800m walking distance of a source of fresh fruits and vegetables (grocery store, supermarket, farmers market, or community-supported agriculture pickup point)
- If no existing source exists within 800m: the development must include provision for a food retail outlet (minimum 100 sqm) or weekly farmers market within the community

### Optimizations

**N02 Food Production**
- Community gardens with minimum 1 plot per 10 dwellings (plot size minimum 9 sqm / 100 sq ft)
- Allotments, urban farms, or productive landscapes integrated into open space system
- Educational programming: gardening workshops, nutrition education, cooking demonstrations (minimum 4 events per year)
- Edible landscaping: fruit trees, herb gardens, berry bushes integrated into streetscape and public spaces

**N03 Healthy Food Retail**
- Community food retail strategy encouraging healthy options: minimum 30% of food retail floor area dedicated to fresh produce, dairy, and whole foods
- Restrict or discourage unhealthy food environments: no fast-food drive-throughs within 400m of schools; limit density of fast-food establishments
- Community nutrition programs: subsidized farm box deliveries for low-income residents, cooking classes, food waste reduction programs

**N04 Food Waste Reduction**
- Community composting facilities within 200m of all dwellings (centralized or distributed)
- Food waste collection service (separate bin, weekly collection) for all residential and commercial units
- Community food sharing infrastructure (community fridges, food banks, surplus redistribution programs)

## Concept 4: Light (L)

**Intent**: Ensure appropriate lighting in outdoor environments to support visual comfort, safety, circadian health, and ecological health.

### Preconditions

**L01 Outdoor Lighting Quality** (Precondition)
- All primary pedestrian routes illuminated to a minimum of 10 lux (horizontal illuminance at ground level)
- All secondary pedestrian routes illuminated to a minimum of 5 lux
- Parks and open spaces illuminated to minimum 3 lux on primary paths, with acceptable dark zones for ecological sensitivity
- Uniformity ratio (minimum-to-average) of 0.25 or better on all pedestrian routes
- All outdoor lighting uses full-cutoff fixtures (no direct uplight) to minimize sky glow and glare

### Optimizations

**L02 Circadian-Supportive Lighting**
- Outdoor lighting color temperature: maximum 3000K (warm white) for all pedestrian and residential area lighting
- Adaptive lighting: dimming capability on all street and area lighting (reduce intensity by 50%+ during low-activity hours, typically 23:00-05:00)
- Daylight access: 75%+ of dwellings achieve minimum 2 hours of direct sunlight on at least one window on the winter solstice (in Northern Hemisphere contexts; equivalent in Southern Hemisphere)

**L03 Glare Control**
- All outdoor luminaires rated BUG (Backlight-Uplight-Glare) with G0, G1, or G2 ratings (minimal glare)
- No unshielded floodlights visible from residential windows
- Decorative and accent lighting limited to avoid light trespass: maximum 1 lux at any residential window from exterior sources

**L04 Daylight in Public Spaces**
- Public spaces (plazas, parks, playgrounds) designed to receive minimum 4 hours of direct sunlight on the equinox (March 21 / September 21) on 50%+ of the space area
- Shadow studies required for all buildings above 4 stories demonstrating compliance

**L05 Ecological Lighting**
- Dark sky zones in parks and ecological corridors: maximum 1 lux on paths, no direct illumination of vegetation canopy, water bodies, or habitat areas
- Lighting curfew in ecological zones: exterior lighting dimmed or extinguished between 23:00-05:00
- Bird-safe lighting: no high-intensity uplighting, no decorative searchlights, warm color temperature (<2700K) near habitat areas

## Concept 5: Movement (M)

**Intent**: Promote physical activity through active transportation infrastructure, recreation facilities, and environmental design that encourages movement throughout the day.

### Preconditions

**M01 Pedestrian Network** (Precondition)
- Continuous, connected pedestrian network with sidewalks on both sides of all streets (minimum 1.8m clear width, 2.4m recommended, 3.0m on commercial streets)
- Accessible routes (ADA/DDA compliant) connecting all dwellings to transit stops, schools, parks, and community facilities
- Maximum block perimeter of 500m (400m preferred); mid-block pedestrian passages where blocks exceed 150m
- All pedestrian crossings at intersections marked, lit, and accessible (curb ramps, tactile paving)

**M02 Active Recreation** (Precondition)
- Outdoor recreation facilities (playground, sports court, fitness trail, swimming pool, sports field) within 800m walking distance of 90%+ of dwellings
- Indoor recreation/fitness facility within 1200m walking distance of 90%+ of dwellings
- All recreation facilities are publicly accessible (not gated or restricted to members only) or equivalent public facilities are available within stated distances

### Optimizations

**M03 Cycling Infrastructure**
- Connected cycling network with protected bike lanes on all streets with design speed above 30 km/h or traffic volume above 3,000 AADT
- Secure covered bicycle storage for every dwelling (minimum 1 per bedroom, maximum 2 per dwelling for houses, 1.5 per dwelling for apartments)
- Short-term visitor cycle parking at community facilities, retail, and public spaces (minimum 2 stands per facility)
- Bike-share/hire station within 400m of all dwellings
- Bicycle maintenance station (pump, tools) at community facilities

**M04 Active Transportation to Transit**
- Walking and cycling routes from all parts of the community to transit stops designed to be direct, safe, well-lit, and attractive (no route more than 20% longer than the straight-line distance)
- Covered waiting areas at transit stops with seating, lighting, and real-time information
- Secure cycle parking at transit stations (minimum 20 spaces per station)
- Wayfinding signage on all routes to transit showing walking time and distance

**M05 Active Design Features**
- Stairways in community buildings designed as attractive, visible, and convenient alternatives to elevators: located prominently, well-lit, artwork or views, wider than minimum code (1.5m+), music or visual interest
- Walking trails and fitness loops within the community open space system (marked distance, varied terrain, outdoor fitness equipment stations every 200-400m)
- Active play infrastructure: natural play areas, parkour/calisthenics parks, outdoor table tennis, climbing walls

**M06 Pedestrian-Priority Streets**
- 20%+ of the community street network designed as pedestrian-priority (shared streets, woonerven, play streets, car-free zones)
- At least one car-free zone of minimum 0.5 ha in the community center, accessible to all residents within 800m
- Temporary street closures for community events (play streets, markets, festivals) at least monthly

## Concept 6: Thermal Comfort (T)

**Intent**: Ensure comfortable thermal conditions in outdoor public spaces throughout the year.

### Preconditions

**T01 Thermal Resilience** (Precondition)
- A thermal comfort assessment has been prepared for key public spaces, evaluating conditions (Universal Thermal Climate Index or Physiological Equivalent Temperature) during: summer peak (hottest month average afternoon), winter nadir (coldest month average midday), and shoulder seasons
- Mitigation measures are incorporated to maintain thermal comfort conditions in public spaces for at least 8 months of the year

### Optimizations

**T02 Shade Provision**
- 40%+ of sidewalk area shaded by tree canopy, building canopy, or shade structures during peak summer hours (11:00-15:00 local time)
- 50%+ of playground area shaded (trees or shade sails/structures)
- 30%+ of primary public space area (plazas, squares) shaded during peak summer hours
- Tree species selected for appropriate canopy density (deciduous for seasonal solar access, evergreen for year-round shade in tropical climates)

**T03 Cool Surfaces**
- 50%+ of non-roof hardscape uses cool materials (solar reflectance index SRI 29 or higher) or permeable pavement
- 75%+ of roof area on community buildings uses cool roof materials (SRI 82+ low-slope, SRI 39+ steep-slope) or green roof
- Cool pavement technologies on 30%+ of roadway surface (reflective coatings, lighter-colored aggregates, permeable surface)

**T04 Evaporative Cooling**
- Water features (fountains, mist systems, shallow water play) in primary public spaces for evaporative cooling during hot months
- Irrigation of public green spaces during heat events (not counted as waste; considered as health intervention)
- Green infrastructure (bioswales, rain gardens, planted areas) designed to provide evapotranspiration cooling effect

**T05 Urban Heat Island Mitigation**
- Community-wide urban heat island reduction strategy: tree canopy target 30%+ at maturity, green roofs on 30%+ of roof area, cool surfaces, green corridors, water features
- Heat vulnerability mapping identifying highest-risk areas (elderly housing, schools, health facilities) and prioritizing mitigation in those areas
- Cool refuge network: air-conditioned community buildings (library, community center, retail) accessible within 400m of all dwellings during extreme heat events

## Concept 7: Sound (S)

**Intent**: Protect community members from harmful noise exposure and promote positive acoustic environments.

### Preconditions

**S01 Noise Exposure Assessment** (Precondition)
- A noise assessment has been prepared identifying all significant noise sources affecting the community and predicting noise levels at all dwellings and public spaces
- Noise-sensitive uses (residential, schools, healthcare, parks) are designed to meet WHO Community Noise Guidelines: outdoor living areas 55 dB LAeq,16h (day), bedrooms with windows closed 30 dB LAeq,8h (night), bedrooms with windows open 45 dB LAeq,8h (night)

### Optimizations

**S02 Noise Mitigation**
- Spatial planning measures: noise-sensitive uses set back from noise sources, non-sensitive uses (commercial, parking, community buildings) used as barriers, buildings oriented with bedrooms facing away from noise sources
- Acoustic barriers: where noise levels exceed guidelines, earth berms (2-4m), acoustic fences (2-4m), or building facades designed as acoustic barriers
- Traffic noise reduction: speed limits of 30 km/h or lower on residential streets, low-noise road surfaces, electric vehicle priority zones

**S03 Positive Soundscapes**
- Design public spaces with positive acoustic qualities: water features masking traffic noise, vegetation providing natural sound (rustling leaves, bird attraction planting), spatial design creating quiet zones away from traffic
- Sound level targets for key public spaces: park quiet areas 45 dB LAeq or lower, plazas 55 dB LAeq or lower
- Sound walks and acoustic art installations as part of public realm programming

## Concept 8: Materials (MA)

**Intent**: Reduce human exposure to hazardous materials in the community environment and promote responsible material selection.

### Preconditions

**MA01 Hazardous Material Management** (Precondition)
- If the site has a history of contamination (brownfield): Phase I and Phase II Environmental Site Assessments completed; remediation plan approved and implemented; post-remediation verification confirming contaminant levels below applicable residential or commercial standards
- Asbestos-containing materials in any retained buildings identified and managed (removal or encapsulation per regulatory requirements)
- Lead paint in any retained buildings identified and managed

### Optimizations

**MA02 Responsible Material Selection**
- Community infrastructure materials (roads, sidewalks, utilities, street furniture) prioritize: recycled content (30%+ by value), responsibly sourced timber (FSC or equivalent), low-VOC coatings and sealants, durable and low-maintenance materials to reduce lifecycle impacts
- Material health transparency: 20%+ of permanently installed products (by cost) have Health Product Declarations (HPD) or Declare labels

**MA03 Construction Material Health**
- Restrict the use of materials containing substances on the ILFI Red List or equivalent (asbestos, lead, mercury, PVC, formaldehyde-added products, halogenated flame retardants) in public realm and community buildings
- Playground equipment and surfaces: free of heavy metals, PVC, and phthalates; safety surfacing meets EN 1177 or ASTM F1292 for impact attenuation

## Concept 9: Mind (D)

**Intent**: Support mental health and cognitive well-being through environmental design, nature access, and community programming.

### Preconditions

**D01 Nature Access** (Precondition)
- 90%+ of dwellings within 300m walking distance of a green space (park, garden, green corridor) of minimum 0.1 ha
- 90%+ of dwellings within 800m walking distance of a substantial green space of minimum 1.0 ha
- Green spaces must be publicly accessible, maintained, and include: seating, shade, planting, and at least one natural element (water, meadow, woodland)

### Optimizations

**D02 Biophilic Design**
- Community public spaces incorporate biophilic design principles: direct nature (plants, water, animals, natural light, natural ventilation), indirect nature (natural materials, nature patterns, natural colors, nature views), and experience of space (prospect, refuge, mystery, complexity)
- Street trees on 80%+ of streets (both sides, maximum 9m spacing)
- Green walls or facades on community buildings (minimum 1 per 10,000 sqm of built area)
- Biodiversity-focused planting: native species, pollinator-friendly, wildlife corridors

**D03 Stress Reduction**
- Quiet contemplation spaces: at least one per 500 dwellings, minimum 200 sqm, designed for solitude and reflection (seating, shade, nature sounds, no amplified music, no active recreation), maintained as a quiet zone
- Therapeutic landscapes: sensory gardens, meditation gardens, or healing gardens at healthcare facilities, senior housing, and community centers
- Views of nature: 75%+ of dwellings have views of trees, vegetation, or water from at least one habitable room (living room or bedroom)

**D04 Community Mental Health Support**
- Mental health first aid training available to community staff (property management, security, recreation staff) -- minimum 1 trained person per 500 residents
- Community wellness programming: yoga, tai chi, meditation, mindfulness classes in public spaces or community buildings (minimum weekly)
- Information on local mental health services displayed at community buildings and on community communications platforms

## Concept 10: Community (C)

**Intent**: Foster social cohesion, civic engagement, equity, and a sense of belonging.

### Preconditions

**C01 Community Engagement** (Precondition)
- A community engagement strategy is in place for the planning, design, and management phases
- Engagement activities include: public meetings, design workshops, online surveys, door-to-door outreach, and targeted engagement with underrepresented groups (youth, elderly, disabled, ethnic minorities, low-income)
- Documentation of how engagement outcomes have influenced design decisions

### Optimizations

**C02 Inclusive Design**
- Universal design features in public realm: accessible routes (wheelchair, stroller, mobility aid), seating every 50-100m on primary routes (with armrests and backs), accessible play equipment in all playgrounds, accessible community garden plots (raised beds), multi-sensory wayfinding (visual, tactile, auditory)
- Gender-inclusive design: well-lit routes, natural surveillance, mixed-use public spaces (not single-function), flexible programming that serves diverse user groups

**C03 Social Interaction**
- Design public spaces to promote casual social interaction: benches facing each other (not just parallel to path), community notice boards, communal seating areas at building entries, shared facilities (laundry, workshop, kitchen, guest room), ground-floor communal rooms in apartment buildings
- Programming: community events calendar with minimum monthly events (markets, festivals, film screenings, sports tournaments, seasonal celebrations)

**C04 Civic and Cultural Spaces**
- Community meeting rooms available for public booking (minimum 1 per 500 dwellings, minimum 50 sqm each, free or subsidized for community groups)
- Cultural programming: public art (1-2% of infrastructure budget), performance space (outdoor stage or indoor auditorium), exhibition space, community library or reading room

**C05 Safety and Security**
- Crime Prevention Through Environmental Design (CPTED) principles applied: natural surveillance (active ground floors, windows overlooking public spaces), natural access control (clear public/private boundaries), territorial reinforcement (community identity, ownership cues), maintenance (prompt repair, no graffiti, clean environments)
- Community safety plan: 24/7 emergency communication (call points, mobile app), community watch program (voluntary, not exclusionary), well-lit emergency routes

**C06 Digital Connectivity**
- High-speed internet access (minimum 100 Mbps download) available to all dwellings and public spaces
- Free public Wi-Fi in primary public spaces (parks, plazas, community buildings, transit stops)
- Digital inclusion: device lending library, digital literacy classes, assisted access for elderly and disabled residents

**C07 Emergency Preparedness**
- Community emergency preparedness plan addressing: natural disasters (flood, earthquake, wildfire, storm), extreme weather (heat, cold), infrastructure failure (power outage, water disruption), public health emergencies
- Emergency supply caches at community buildings (water, first aid, blankets, communications equipment)
- Community notification system (SMS, app, siren) tested annually

**C08 Affordability and Equity**
- Housing affordability: minimum 15% of dwelling units affordable at 80% AMI or below (or local equivalent)
- Affordable commercial space: minimum 10% of commercial floor area offered at below-market rents for local businesses, social enterprises, or community organizations
- Equitable access: all community facilities, parks, and services accessible to all residents regardless of income, age, or ability; no gated areas or exclusive amenities

## Concept 11: Innovation (I)

**Intent**: Reward innovative strategies that advance human health and well-being beyond the requirements of the standard.

### Optimizations

**I01 Innovation Features**
- Projects may earn additional points for innovative strategies that:
  - Address a health issue not covered by the 10 core concepts
  - Exceed the highest performance threshold of any existing optimization by a significant margin
  - Demonstrate a novel approach to health promotion in community design
  - Pilot a WELL feature or strategy under development by IWBI
- Each innovation must be documented with: narrative description, health rationale (citing peer-reviewed research), implementation plan, and measurable performance metrics
- Maximum of 10 innovation points available

**Common Innovation Strategies for Communities**:
- Zero-carbon community energy system
- Community health metrics dashboard (real-time tracking of air quality, noise, activity levels, water quality)
- Comprehensive food system plan (from production through waste, addressing food deserts and nutritional access)
- Climate-adapted design strategy (heat action plan, flood resilience, wildfire protection)
- Regenerative landscape plan (biodiversity net gain, carbon sequestration, ecosystem services quantification)
- Health impact assessment (HIA) integrated into the planning and design process
- Social prescribing program partnership (connecting residents to nature, physical activity, and social connection through healthcare providers)
- Age-friendly community design (WHO Age-Friendly Cities framework integration)

## Cross-Reference: WELL Community and LEED-ND Synergies

Projects pursuing both WELL Community and LEED-ND can achieve significant overlap:

| WELL Concept | Overlapping LEED-ND Credits | Shared Design Strategy |
|-------------|---------------------------|----------------------|
| Air (A) | GIB-17 Light Pollution Reduction (related: dark sky = less energy = less emissions) | Clean energy, construction management, vegetation buffers |
| Water (W) | GIB-3 Indoor Water, GIB-4 Outdoor Water, GIB-8 Rainwater | Water efficiency, green infrastructure, stormwater management |
| Nourishment (N) | NPD-13 Local Food Production | Community gardens, farmers markets, food retail access |
| Light (L) | GIB-17 Light Pollution, GIB-10 Solar Orientation | Dark sky lighting, solar orientation, daylight access |
| Movement (M) | NPD-1 Walkable Streets, NPD-6 Connected Community, SLL-5 Bicycle | Sidewalks, bike infrastructure, recreation, connectivity |
| Thermal (T) | GIB-9 Heat Island Reduction | Cool surfaces, tree canopy, green infrastructure |
| Sound (S) | -- (no direct overlap) | Spatial planning, traffic calming |
| Materials (MA) | SLL-2 Brownfield Remediation, GIB-15 Recycled Infrastructure | Remediation, responsible sourcing |
| Mind (D) | NPD-9 Civic and Public Space, SLL-7/8/9 Habitat | Green space access, nature, biophilic design |
| Community (C) | NPD-12 Community Outreach, NPD-4 Housing Affordability | Engagement, affordability, safety, equity |

### Cost and Timeline

- **Registration fee**: approximately $5,000-15,000 depending on project size
- **Review fees**: approximately $15,000-50,000 for documentation review and performance testing
- **Performance testing**: approximately $10,000-25,000 for on-site measurements (air, water, light, sound)
- **Timeline**: 12-24 months from registration to certification
- **Recertification**: every 3 years, approximately 50% of initial certification cost
- **WELL AP**: at least one WELL Accredited Professional on the team is recommended (not mandatory but provides a credential for the project team)
