# Transect Guide: Complete T1-T6 Reference

Comprehensive reference for the Transect system (T1-T6) with all dimensional and use standards derived from the SmartCode v9.2, frontage type catalog, building disposition types, and special district overlays. This document provides the complete technical specifications needed to calibrate a transect-based code for any project.

---

## 1. Transect Zone Detailed Specifications

### T1 Natural Zone

**Intent**: To preserve lands in a natural or wilderness condition, including lands unsuitable for human settlement due to topography, hydrology, vegetation, or wildlife habitat. T1 lands provide ecosystem services essential to human settlement: water filtration, flood attenuation, carbon sequestration, biodiversity support, air purification, and recreational opportunity.

**Dimensional Standards**:
| Parameter | Standard |
|-----------|---------|
| Building coverage | 0-2% of total T1 area (conservation structures only) |
| Impervious surface | Maximum 5% |
| Building height | 1 story / 5m maximum |
| Setback from water bodies | 30m minimum (50m for tidal waters) |
| Setback from wetlands | 15m minimum |
| Setback from bluff/steep slope | 15m minimum from top of slope |
| Density | 0 DU/ha (no residential permitted) |
| FAR | N/A |

**Permitted Uses**: Conservation, passive recreation (hiking, birdwatching, nature study), forestry (sustainable harvest only), wildlife management, environmental education, ecological restoration.

**Permitted Structures**: Trails (permeable surface preferred, max 3.0m width), boardwalks over wetlands (1.2-1.8m width), observation platforms (max 30 sqm), restroom facilities at trailheads (composting or vault), signage (interpretive and wayfinding, non-illuminated), small ranger/caretaker station (max 60 sqm).

**Street Types**: Trails, unpaved paths, boardwalks. No vehicular streets except service roads for maintenance and emergency access (unpaved, max 4.0m width).

**Civic Space Types**: Nature preserve, greenway, wildlife corridor, watershed protection area.

**Landscape**: Native vegetation, no introduced species, ecological restoration where degraded, no irrigation, no fertilizers or pesticides, natural drainage patterns maintained.

### T2 Rural Zone

**Intent**: To preserve and enhance sparsely settled lands in open or cultivated state, including woodland, grassland, farmland, and scattered buildings. The landscape is the dominant element; buildings are subsidiary to the natural and agricultural context. T2 provides food production, scenic beauty, rural economy, and a clear edge to the urbanized area.

**Dimensional Standards**:
| Parameter | Standard |
|-----------|---------|
| Minimum lot area | 2 ha (5 acres) agricultural, 0.4 ha (1 acre) residential hamlet |
| Minimum lot width | 30m (hamlet), 60m (agricultural) |
| Maximum lot coverage | 10-20% |
| Maximum building height | 2 stories / 9m (residential), 12m (agricultural structures such as barns and silos) |
| Front setback | 10-30m from road edge |
| Side setback | 5-10m |
| Rear setback | 10-20m |
| Maximum FAR | 0.1-0.3 |
| Maximum density | 0.5-2.5 DU/ha (0.2-1.0 DU/acre) |
| Impervious surface | Maximum 20% of lot |
| Open space (per lot) | Minimum 70% |

**Permitted Uses**: Agriculture (crops, orchards, vineyards, animal husbandry), single-family detached dwelling, accessory dwelling unit (caretaker, farm worker), bed-and-breakfast (max 5 rooms), stables and riding facilities, farm stand (seasonal, max 30 sqm), home occupation, religious institution, cemetery, public park.

**Conditional Uses**: Winery/brewery with tasting room, small-scale event venue (agricultural tourism), kennels, veterinary clinic, small school (max 100 students), utility facility.

**Building Types**: Estate house, farmhouse, cottage, agricultural building (barn, stable, greenhouse, equipment shed), accessory dwelling unit, rural church/meeting house.

**Frontage Types**: Fence (wood, stone, or hedge), naturalistic (no defined edge, landscape transitions to road), porch (agricultural vernacular).

**Street Types**: Rural road (2 lanes, no curbs, open swale drainage, 5.5-6.5m pavement width, 60 km/h design speed), rural avenue (2 lanes with tree-lined shoulders, 6.0-7.0m pavement width), rural path (unpaved, 1.5-2.4m width).

**Civic Space Types**: Greenway, nature park, community garden, farm commons.

**Landscape**: Native and adapted species, agricultural crops, hedgerows, windbreaks, woodland patches, meadows. No formal landscaping except immediately around buildings. Open swale drainage. Dark sky compliance (no upward-directed lighting, shielded fixtures only, maximum 3000K color temperature).

### T3 Sub-Urban Zone

**Intent**: To provide a low-density residential environment with detached buildings, landscaped yards, and tree-lined streets. T3 is the most common zone in most municipalities and represents a walkable, connected version of suburban development with sidewalks, street trees, and proximity to neighborhood centers (T4/T5). Blocks are moderate in size with connected street networks (no cul-de-sacs except where topography demands).

**Dimensional Standards**:
| Parameter | Standard |
|-----------|---------|
| Minimum lot area | 250-600 sqm (varies by building type) |
| Minimum lot width | 12-18m |
| Maximum lot width | 30-45m |
| Maximum lot coverage | 30-50% |
| Maximum building height | 2-3 stories / 12m |
| Ground floor height | 2.7m minimum (residential) |
| Front setback | 5-10m |
| Build-to line compliance | 50% of lot width at front setback line |
| Side setback (interior) | 3-5m |
| Side setback (street-facing) | 3-5m |
| Rear setback | 6-10m |
| Rear setback to alley | 3m |
| Maximum FAR | 0.3-0.8 |
| Maximum density | 5-12 DU/ha (2-5 DU/acre) |
| Open space (per lot) | Minimum 40-60% of lot area |
| Maximum block perimeter | 500-600m |
| Maximum block face length | 150-200m |
| Required street trees | Both sides of all streets, 7.5-9.0m spacing |

**Permitted Uses**: Single-family detached dwelling, duplex (side-by-side or stacked), cottage (max 120 sqm), accessory dwelling unit (max 75 sqm or 50% of principal dwelling), home occupation (max 25% of floor area, no external employees, no signage), daycare (max 8 children), community garden, public park, playground, small civic building (library branch, community center, fire station).

**Conditional Uses**: Triplex, small apartment (max 8 units), small office (max 200 sqm, ground floor only), bed-and-breakfast, religious institution, school (elementary), assisted living (max 20 residents).

**Building Types**:
- **Detached house**: single-family, 1-3 stories, 150-400 sqm, edgeyard disposition, porch or dooryard frontage
- **Cottage**: single-family small-lot, 1-2 stories, 60-120 sqm, edgeyard, porch frontage
- **Duplex**: two dwelling units, 1-3 stories, 200-500 sqm total, edgeyard or sideyard, porch or stoop frontage
- **Villa**: larger single-family, 2-3 stories, 300-600 sqm, edgeyard, porch or forecourt frontage
- **Accessory dwelling unit (ADU)**: accessory to principal dwelling, 1-1.5 stories, max 75 sqm, rearyard

**Frontage Types**: Porch (primary), fence/hedge, dooryard, common yard, forecourt (institutional). See Section 3 for complete frontage catalog.

**Street Types**: Residential street (18-22m ROW), yield street (12-18m ROW), rear lane/alley (4-6m paved width), path (1.5-2.4m width). See Street Design skill for cross-section details.

**Parking**: 1-2 spaces per dwelling unit on-site. Driveway access from alley preferred (rear-loaded). If no alley, front-loaded driveway permitted but limited to 3.0m width at sidewalk (single car) or 5.5m (double). Garage doors must not dominate facade (max 40% of facade width, recessed 0.6m behind facade plane or side-loaded).

**Civic Space Types**: Green, playground, community garden, pocket park, greenway, tot lot.

**Landscape**: Street trees both sides (minimum 7.5m spacing, large-canopy deciduous species). Front yard planted (min 50% of front setback area). Foundation planting at building perimeter. Privacy hedges or fences in side and rear yards (max 1.8m height side, 2.1m rear, max 1.2m in front setback). Native and adapted species preferred. Lawn permitted but limited to 50% of landscaped area (water conservation).

### T4 General Urban Zone

**Intent**: To provide a medium-density, mixed-use environment with a defined streetwall of moderate height. T4 is the "workhorse zone" of a walkable neighborhood, accommodating a wide range of housing types and compatible commercial uses. Buildings have shallow setbacks, creating a sense of spatial enclosure on the street. The zone supports transit viability, walkability, and daily convenience within the neighborhood.

**Dimensional Standards**:
| Parameter | Standard |
|-----------|---------|
| Minimum lot area | 150-400 sqm |
| Minimum lot width | 6-12m |
| Maximum lot width | 24-36m |
| Maximum lot coverage | 50-70% |
| Maximum building height | 2-5 stories / 18m |
| Ground floor height (commercial) | 3.6-4.5m floor-to-floor |
| Ground floor height (residential) | 3.0m floor-to-floor minimum |
| Upper floor height | 2.7-3.6m floor-to-floor |
| Front setback / build-to line | 2-5m from ROW |
| Build-to line compliance | 70% of lot width |
| Side setback (interior) | 0-3m |
| Side setback (street-facing) | 2-4m |
| Rear setback | 3-6m |
| Rear setback to alley | 1.5m |
| Maximum FAR | 1.0-2.5 |
| Maximum density | 12-25 DU/ha (5-10 DU/acre) |
| Open space (per lot) | Minimum 20-40% |
| Maximum block perimeter | 400-500m |
| Maximum block face length | 120-150m |
| Ground-floor transparency (commercial) | 50-60% of street-facing facade |
| Ground-floor transparency (residential) | 25-35% of street-facing facade |

**Permitted Uses**: All residential types (single-family through small apartment, max 20 DU per building), live-work unit, small retail (max 250 sqm per tenant), restaurant (max 200 sqm), cafe, personal services (salon, dry cleaner, tailor), professional office (max 400 sqm), medical/dental office, daycare center, community center, library, religious institution, small hotel/inn (max 30 rooms), public park.

**Conditional Uses**: Larger retail (250-1000 sqm), larger office (400-1000 sqm), bar/lounge, auto repair (fully enclosed, no outdoor storage), gas station (highly contextual), school, parking structure (wrapped with active uses).

**Building Types**:
- **Rowhouse/Townhouse**: 2-4 stories, 4-8m lot width, party walls, 120-300 sqm per unit, sideyard or rearyard disposition, stoop or dooryard frontage
- **Small Apartment Building**: 2-4 stories, 6-20 units, 8-20m lot width, 500-2000 sqm floor area, edgeyard or courtyard disposition, stoop or forecourt frontage
- **Live-Work Unit**: 2-3 stories, ground floor commercial/studio, upper floors residential, 5-8m lot width, shopfront or stoop frontage
- **Duplex/Triplex**: 2-3 stories, 8-15m lot width, sideyard or rearyard disposition, stoop or porch frontage
- **Mixed-Use Building (small)**: 2-4 stories, ground floor retail/office, upper floors residential, 8-20m lot width, shopfront frontage
- **Detached House (where permitted)**: 2-3 stories, 10-18m lot width, edgeyard disposition, porch or dooryard frontage

**Frontage Types**: Stoop (primary residential), dooryard, forecourt, porch, shopfront (commercial ground floor), terrace. See Section 3 for catalog.

**Street Types**: Neighborhood street (15-22m ROW), commercial street (20-27m ROW), avenue (25-35m ROW), rear lane/alley (4-6m paved width).

**Parking**: 0.5-1.5 spaces per dwelling unit. Rear-loaded garages or shared lots via alley access. No front-loaded garages or surface parking between building and street. No parking within 6m of street-facing facade. Bicycle parking: 1 space per 2 DU. Shared parking agreements encouraged for mixed-use projects.

**Civic Space Types**: Square, green, playground, community garden, pocket park, neighborhood park.

### T5 Urban Center Zone

**Intent**: To provide a higher-density, mixed-use environment with consistent streetwall, active ground floors, and the widest variety of uses. T5 is the main street, town center, or downtown of a neighborhood or district. Ground floors are predominantly commercial (retail, restaurant, service); upper floors are residential, office, or hotel. The streetwall is continuous, buildings are built to or near the lot line, and the public realm is highly active and well-maintained.

**Dimensional Standards**:
| Parameter | Standard |
|-----------|---------|
| Minimum lot area | 200-800 sqm |
| Minimum lot width | 6-15m |
| Maximum lot width | 45-60m |
| Maximum lot coverage | 70-90% |
| Maximum building height | 4-8 stories / 28m |
| Ground floor height (commercial) | 4.0-5.0m floor-to-floor |
| Ground floor height (residential, non-required-retail street) | 3.6m floor-to-floor minimum |
| Upper floor height | 2.7-3.6m floor-to-floor |
| Front setback / build-to line | 0-3m from ROW |
| Build-to line compliance | 80% of lot width |
| Side setback (interior) | 0m (party wall) |
| Side setback (street-facing) | 0-3m |
| Rear setback | 3-6m |
| Rear setback to alley | 0m |
| Maximum FAR (base) | 2.5-5.0 |
| Maximum FAR (with bonuses) | 6.0-8.0 |
| Maximum density | 25-60 DU/ha (10-25 DU/acre) |
| Open space (per lot) | Minimum 10-20% (may be rooftop, courtyard, or balcony) |
| Maximum block perimeter | 450-550m |
| Maximum block face length | 130-160m |
| Ground-floor transparency (required retail) | 60-80% of street-facing facade |
| Ground-floor transparency (non-retail) | 40-50% of street-facing facade |
| Ground-floor elevation above sidewalk | 0-0.3m (commercial), 0.6-1.2m (residential, for privacy) |

**Permitted Uses**: All residential types (apartment, condominium, loft), all retail (no size limit except as noted), restaurant, bar/lounge, hotel, office, medical/dental, bank, gallery, theater, health club/gym, daycare center, library, community center, religious institution, government office, transit station.

**Conditional Uses**: Drive-through (strongly discouraged), gas station (prohibited on required retail frontage), auto repair (fully enclosed), large-format retail (>2500 sqm, requires design standards for pedestrian-scale frontage), nightclub/entertainment venue, parking structure (must be lined with active uses on street-facing facades).

**Building Types**:
- **Mid-Rise Apartment/Condominium**: 4-8 stories, 15-45m lot width, 20-80 units, rearyard or courtyard disposition, stoop or terrace frontage (residential streets), shopfront frontage (commercial streets)
- **Mixed-Use Building**: 4-8 stories, ground floor retail/office, upper floors residential/office/hotel, 8-45m lot width, shopfront or gallery frontage
- **Liner Building**: 2-6 stories, wraps a parking structure with habitable space, shopfront frontage at ground, residential or office above
- **Live-Work Building**: 2-4 stories, flexible ground floor, residential above, 6-12m lot width, shopfront frontage
- **Civic Building**: variable, institutional architecture, may have unique form standards per special district

**Frontage Types**: Shopfront (primary commercial), gallery, arcade, terrace, stoop (residential streets only), forecourt (institutional). See Section 3 for catalog.

**Street Types**: Boulevard (30-50m ROW), avenue (25-35m ROW), commercial street (20-27m ROW).

**Parking**: No minimum required (or maximum 0.5 spaces/DU). All parking structured or underground. No surface parking lots between building and any street. Parking structure facades facing streets must be lined with habitable space (min 9m depth). Parking entrance limited to one per block face, max 7.5m wide. Bicycle parking: 1 space per 4 DU (residential), 1 per 500 sqm (commercial), minimum 6 spaces at any commercial establishment.

**Civic Space Types**: Plaza, square, urban park, promenade, pocket park.

### T6 Urban Core Zone

**Intent**: To provide the highest-density, most intense urban environment with the greatest variety of uses, tallest buildings, and most significant civic presence. T6 applies only to the centers of large cities and major regional downtowns. Buildings are towers or high-rise structures on podiums. The public realm is formal, hardscaped, and intensely activated. Transit infrastructure is extensive. This zone represents the pinnacle of urban intensity.

**Dimensional Standards**:
| Parameter | Standard |
|-----------|---------|
| Minimum lot area | 500-2000 sqm |
| Minimum lot width | 15-30m |
| Maximum lot width | 90-120m |
| Maximum lot coverage (podium) | 80-100% |
| Maximum lot coverage (tower) | 40-50% of lot area |
| Maximum building height (podium/streetwall) | 4-8 stories / 18-28m |
| Maximum building height (tower) | Varies by sub-zone (6-30+ stories, 24-120+ meters) |
| Maximum tower floor plate | 750-2000 sqm (smaller plates preferred for light, air, view) |
| Tower separation (same site) | 24m minimum face-to-face |
| Tower separation (adjacent sites) | 24-30m |
| Ground floor height | 4.5-6.0m floor-to-floor |
| Upper floor height (podium) | 3.3-4.5m floor-to-floor |
| Upper floor height (tower) | 2.7-3.6m floor-to-floor |
| Front setback / build-to line | 0-2m from ROW |
| Build-to line compliance | 90% of lot width |
| Side setback (interior) | 0m (podium), per tower separation (tower) |
| Rear setback | 3-6m |
| Step-back above podium | 3.0-6.0m from street-facing podium facade |
| Maximum FAR (base) | 5.0-15.0 |
| Maximum FAR (with bonuses) | 15.0-25.0 |
| Maximum density | 60-150+ DU/ha (25-60+ DU/acre) |
| Open space | 5-15% (rooftop, terrace, sky garden, atrium may count) |
| Ground-floor transparency | 70-90% of street-facing facade |

**Permitted Uses**: All uses permitted. Highest-intensity commercial (department store, convention center, major hotel), Class A office, high-rise residential, cultural institutions (museum, performing arts center, concert hall), government buildings, transit stations and terminals, major medical facilities, university buildings.

**Building Types**:
- **Tower (Point Tower)**: slender tower on a podium, floor plate under 1000 sqm, residential or office, 10-60+ stories
- **Tower (Slab Tower)**: wider tower, floor plate 1000-2000 sqm, office or mixed-use, typically with floorplate articulation to reduce visual bulk
- **Mid-Rise (on podium)**: 6-12 stories, builds out full lot coverage, common in early phases before market supports towers
- **Civic Building**: courthouse, city hall, library, museum, performing arts center, may have unique form per SD overlay
- **Mixed-Use Complex**: multi-building development with towers, podiums, retail base, structured parking, public spaces, connected by internal circulation

**Frontage Types**: Shopfront (primary), gallery, arcade. Stoop and porch not appropriate for T6.

**Street Types**: Boulevard, avenue, commercial street, passage (pedestrian-only through-block connection, 3-6m width).

**Parking**: No minimum required. Structured or underground only. No surface parking anywhere. Parking structure facades must be lined with habitable or commercial space on all street-facing and publicly visible sides (minimum 9m depth). Bicycle parking: 1 space per 4 DU, 1 per 500 sqm commercial, secure indoor storage required for residential.

**Civic Space Types**: Civic plaza, urban plaza, promenade, passage, urban park, atrium (indoor public space, climate-controlled, counts as civic space if publicly accessible during business hours).

## 2. Frontage Type Catalog

Frontage types define the architectural and spatial relationship between a building and the public right-of-way. They are the most visible and experientially important element of the built environment for pedestrians.

### 2.1 Shopfront

**Description**: A ground-floor facade with large glazed openings for retail display, consisting of a display window (large panes), a transom window (above the display window, may be operable for ventilation), a signage band/fascia (between transom and upper facade), a bulkhead/kickplate (below the display window, 300-600mm, durable material), and an entrance door (recessed 0.3-0.9m from facade plane, or flush).

**Dimensions**:
- Setback from lot line: 0-0.6m
- Transparency: 60-80% of ground-floor facade area
- Display window sill height: 300-600mm above sidewalk
- Transom height: above door head (2.4-3.0m) to ceiling/structure (3.6-4.5m)
- Signage band: 300-600mm height, within building facade plane
- Minimum clear height at entrance: 2.4m
- Entrance recess depth: 0.3-0.9m (provides weather protection and visual cue)
- Awning/canopy projection: 1.2-2.4m from facade, min 2.4m clearance above sidewalk

**Applicability**: T4 (commercial streets only), T5, T6. Required on designated retail frontage streets.

**Design Standards**:
- No blank walls exceeding 3.0m in length at ground floor
- No opaque or reflective glazing (clear glass, min 70% visible light transmittance)
- Display windows must be illuminated during business hours
- Security grilles, if required, must be interior and at least 75% transparent (open mesh)
- Roll-down metal shutters covering the full storefront are prohibited

### 2.2 Gallery

**Description**: A covered walkway created by a colonnade or post-and-beam structure attached to the building facade and extending over the public sidewalk. The gallery is open on three sides (front and two ends) and provides continuous weather protection along the building frontage. The building facade behind the gallery may be shopfront, residential entrance, or office entrance.

**Dimensions**:
- Depth (clear, from facade to column face): 2.4-3.6m
- Clear height: 3.0-4.5m (minimum 3.0m at lowest point of any beam or ceiling)
- Column spacing: 3.0-6.0m on center
- Column dimensions: 300-450mm square or diameter (masonry, steel, or timber)
- Gallery floor: at sidewalk level, same material and grade as public sidewalk
- Railing at gallery edge: not required (open to sidewalk)
- Upper floors may project to column line (building above gallery occupies the gallery depth)

**Applicability**: T4 (commercial), T5, T6. Especially appropriate in warm and rainy climates.

**Design Standards**:
- Gallery must be continuous along the full lot frontage (no gaps except at vehicular entrances)
- Gallery ceiling must be finished (painted plaster, wood, or equivalent -- no exposed structure)
- Lighting: recessed or pendant fixtures in gallery ceiling, minimum 10 lux on gallery floor
- Gallery columns must align with adjacent property gallery columns where feasible
- Gallery depth must be consistent along the block face

### 2.3 Arcade

**Description**: A covered walkway within the building volume. Unlike a gallery (which is an addition projecting from the facade), an arcade is created by setting the ground-floor facade back from the lot line while maintaining the upper floors at the lot line. The upper floors overhang the ground-floor setback, creating a covered passage. The arcade is part of the building structure.

**Dimensions**:
- Depth: 2.4-3.6m (measured from upper-floor facade plane to ground-floor facade)
- Clear height: 3.0-4.5m
- Column or pier spacing: 3.0-6.0m
- Column dimensions: 300-600mm (often more substantial than gallery columns as they support upper floors)
- Arcade floor: at sidewalk level, may be a higher-quality material than public sidewalk
- Arcade ceiling height: 3.6-4.5m preferred (grander scale than gallery)

**Applicability**: T5, T6. Common in Mediterranean, Latin American, and Asian urbanism.

**Design Standards**:
- Arcade must be open to the public during business hours
- No barriers, gates, or ropes restricting access to arcade from public sidewalk
- Arcade ceiling: vaulted, coffered, or flat with decorative treatment
- Lighting integrated into ceiling or walls
- Ground-floor uses behind arcade must activate the arcade (shopfronts, restaurant entrances, lobby entrances)

### 2.4 Stoop

**Description**: An elevated entry platform with stairs leading from the sidewalk to the building entrance. The stoop provides a semi-private transition zone between the public sidewalk and the private dwelling. It is the quintessential residential frontage type of urban rowhouses and townhouses.

**Dimensions**:
- Depth (platform): 0.6-1.8m
- Width: 1.2-2.4m (may span the full lot width for wider stoops)
- Height above sidewalk: 0.6-1.5m (3-8 risers)
- Stair width: 0.9-1.5m
- Stair orientation: perpendicular to facade (most common) or parallel (for narrow lots)
- Railing: required when height exceeds 0.6m, 0.9-1.1m height, open design (not solid wall)
- Landing at door: minimum 0.9m x 1.2m clear

**Applicability**: T3 (where appropriate), T4, T5 (residential streets only). Not appropriate for T6 or commercial frontage streets.

**Design Standards**:
- Stoop must face the primary street (not the side or rear)
- Building entrance must be at stoop level (no entry at sidewalk level with interior stairs)
- Stoop may include a small roof or canopy (1.2-1.8m projection)
- House number and lighting at stoop entrance
- Under-stoop space may accommodate utility meters, storage, or building access (screened)

### 2.5 Porch

**Description**: A covered, unenclosed room attached to the building facade, elevated slightly above the sidewalk, providing a semi-public social space where residents can see and be seen from the street. The porch is the quintessential frontage type of American residential neighborhoods and small towns.

**Dimensions**:
- Depth: 1.8-3.0m clear (measured from facade to porch edge)
- Width: minimum 50% of facade width (typically full width of facade)
- Height above sidewalk: 0.3-0.9m (2-5 risers)
- Clear height (floor to ceiling): full story height (2.7-3.6m)
- Column spacing: 2.4-3.6m
- Column dimensions: 200-300mm
- Railing: 0.9-1.1m height, open design (baluster spacing max 100mm)
- Roof: shed, hip, or gable, integrated with building roof

**Applicability**: T3, T4. Not appropriate for T5, T6.

**Design Standards**:
- Porch must face the primary street
- Porch may wrap around corner on corner lots (two-sided porch)
- Porch may not be enclosed with glass, screen (seasonal screen in temperate climates may be permitted), or solid walls
- Porch depth must accommodate comfortable seating (minimum 1.8m)
- Ceiling fan, porch light, and house number required
- Porch columns must be proportional to building scale

### 2.6 Forecourt

**Description**: A portion of the lot left open between the building facade and the lot line, creating a small semi-public court or garden. The building wings typically wrap around the sides of the forecourt, creating a U-shaped or L-shaped plan. The forecourt functions as an entry garden, outdoor dining area, or civic gathering space.

**Dimensions**:
- Depth: 3.0-6.0m (measured from lot line to building facade at deepest point)
- Width: 6.0-15.0m (typically the full lot width or a portion defined by building wings)
- Enclosure: building walls on two or three sides (U-shape or L-shape)
- Edge treatment at lot line: low wall (0.3-0.9m), fence, hedge, or bollards defining the forecourt edge without fully enclosing it
- Surface: high-quality paving (stone, unit pavers, brick), planted areas, seating

**Applicability**: T3 (institutional), T4, T5. Appropriate for apartment buildings, hotels, restaurants, institutional buildings.

**Design Standards**:
- Forecourt must be visible and accessible from the public sidewalk (no solid walls or locked gates during business hours)
- Forecourt may include fountain, sculpture, or landscape feature
- Outdoor dining permitted in forecourt with appropriate furniture
- Lighting: pedestrian-scale (3.0-4.0m height), warm (3000K), minimum 5 lux

### 2.7 Common Yard

**Description**: The building is set back substantially from the lot line, with the front yard functioning as a shared community green or garden. Common yards are typically associated with cottage courts, co-housing communities, or campus-style development where multiple buildings share a central green.

**Dimensions**:
- Depth: 5.0-10.0m (measured from lot line to nearest building facade)
- Width: typically the full lot width or development parcel width
- Surface: predominantly planted (lawn, garden beds, trees), with paths (1.2-1.8m paved or gravel)
- Enclosure: defined by building facades on two or more sides, lot line edge may be open or have low fence/hedge (max 0.9m)

**Applicability**: T3. May be appropriate for T4 in some cottage court or co-housing contexts.

### 2.8 Dooryard

**Description**: A shallow planted or paved area between the building facade and the sidewalk, defined by a low wall, fence, or hedge. The dooryard provides a thin private buffer at the building entrance without a porch or stoop. Common in village and cottage contexts.

**Dimensions**:
- Depth: 0.6-2.4m
- Edge treatment: low wall (0.3-0.6m), picket fence (0.6-1.2m), or clipped hedge (0.6-1.2m)
- Surface: planted (perennials, groundcover) or paved (stone, brick)
- Entry: gate in wall/fence at building entrance, 0.9-1.2m wide

**Applicability**: T3, T4 (residential streets).

### 2.9 Terrace

**Description**: The building is set back from the lot line with an elevated terrace or platform in front, often with a retaining wall or balustrade at the sidewalk edge. The terrace is above sidewalk grade, providing privacy and a sense of elevation for the building entrance. Common on sloping sites.

**Dimensions**:
- Depth: 1.2-3.0m
- Height above sidewalk: 0.3-1.0m
- Retaining wall/balustrade: 0.3-1.0m at sidewalk edge (open balustrade if above 0.6m for safety)
- Surface: stone, concrete, or unit pavers
- Stairs: at building entrance, 0.9-1.5m wide, perpendicular to sidewalk

**Applicability**: T4, T5 (residential streets). Appropriate for sloping sites.

### 2.10 Lightwell / Areaway

**Description**: A below-grade court in front of the building, providing light and access to an occupied basement level. The lightwell is protected by a railing or low wall at sidewalk level. Common in dense urban buildings with lower-level commercial or residential space.

**Dimensions**:
- Depth below sidewalk: 1.2-3.0m (full story below grade)
- Width: 1.2-3.0m (measured perpendicular to facade)
- Length: 3.0-6.0m (along facade)
- Railing at sidewalk level: 0.9-1.1m height, open design
- Access stair width: 0.9m minimum, with handrail
- Drainage: required (floor drain connected to storm system)

**Applicability**: T5, T6. Common in older urban areas, brownstones, and buildings with basement retail or residential.

## 3. Building Disposition Types

Building disposition describes how a building is placed on its lot relative to the lot lines and open space. The four primary dispositions are:

### 3.1 Edgeyard

**Description**: The building is set back from all lot lines, surrounded by open space on all sides (front yard, side yards, rear yard). This is the most common suburban disposition.

**Characteristics**: Detached building, visible from all sides, yard on all sides, requires finished architecture on all four facades, driveway typically from front or side street.

**Applicability**: T2, T3. May occur in T4 for detached houses on larger lots.

**Typical Building Types**: Detached house, cottage, villa, estate house, church, school.

### 3.2 Sideyard

**Description**: The building occupies one side of the lot, with the open space on the opposite side. The building has a party wall or near-zero setback on one side and a substantial side yard on the other. This creates a rhythm of solid and void along the street.

**Characteristics**: Building against one side lot line (or with minimal setback), open space on opposite side, can create paired-house configurations (two sideyard buildings flanking a shared garden), requires finished architecture on three facades.

**Applicability**: T3, T4. Common for Charleston-type side-hall houses, sideyard cottages.

**Typical Building Types**: Sideyard house (Charleston single house), semi-detached house, cottage.

### 3.3 Rearyard

**Description**: The building occupies the front portion of the lot, with open space in the rear. The building is built to or near the front lot line and side lot lines, with a rear yard or courtyard behind. This is the classic urban building disposition.

**Characteristics**: Building at front of lot (at or near BTL), party walls on one or both sides, rear yard provides light and air, can create perimeter block configuration when all lots on a block have rearyard disposition.

**Applicability**: T4, T5, T6. The primary disposition for urban buildings.

**Typical Building Types**: Rowhouse, townhouse, apartment building, mixed-use building, commercial building.

### 3.4 Courtyard

**Description**: The building occupies all edges of the lot, enclosing an interior courtyard that provides light, air, and private open space. The building is built to lot lines on all sides with an opening or passage to the courtyard from the street.

**Characteristics**: Building on all lot lines, interior courtyard (minimum 6m x 6m for light and air), passage to courtyard from street (min 1.8m width, max 3.6m), upper-level balconies and galleries overlooking courtyard, most private of the dispositions.

**Applicability**: T4, T5, T6. Common in Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, and Latin American urbanism.

**Typical Building Types**: Courtyard apartment, courtyard house, courtyard commercial building, carriage house configuration.

## 4. Special District Overlays

Special Districts (SD) modify or replace the underlying transect zone standards for areas with unique conditions or objectives.

### SD-Civic (Civic District)

**Intent**: Accommodate civic and institutional buildings that may require unique form, larger footprints, or distinctive architecture not typical of the surrounding transect zone.

**Modified Standards**:
- Building height: may exceed underlying zone by 1-2 stories for civic prominence
- Setback: may be increased to create civic forecourts, ceremonial approaches
- FAR: may exceed underlying zone
- Frontage: may have unique frontage (monumental entrance, colonnade, formal garden)
- Architecture: may be exempt from architectural standards to allow distinctive civic design
- Parking: reduced requirements (public transit access emphasized)

**Typical Buildings**: City hall, courthouse, library, museum, performing arts center, convention center, public school, fire station, police station, post office, transit station.

### SD-Waterfront (Waterfront District)

**Intent**: Manage development along rivers, lakes, harbors, and coastlines with standards that ensure public access to the water's edge, flood resilience, environmental protection, and maritime character.

**Modified Standards**:
- Public access: continuous public waterfront promenade required (min 6.0m width, 10.0m preferred)
- Setback from water's edge: 15-30m for buildings (promenade + landscape buffer)
- Building orientation: primary facades and entrances facing waterfront promenade
- Ground-floor uses: maritime commercial (restaurant, cafe, marine supply, boat rental), public facilities (fishing pier, kayak launch, viewing platform)
- Flood resilience: minimum finished floor elevation per FEMA + 1.0m freeboard
- Environmental: stormwater treatment before discharge, riparian buffer, native shoreline plantings
- View corridors: view corridors to water preserved at minimum every 100m along waterfront

### SD-Transit (Transit-Oriented District)

**Intent**: Maximize density, connectivity, and transit ridership within walking distance of major transit stations (rail, BRT, ferry).

**Modified Standards**:
- Density bonus: 1.5-3x the underlying zone FAR within 400m of station entrance
- Parking: no minimum, maximum 0.5 spaces/DU within 200m of station, maximum 0.75 spaces/DU within 200-400m
- Pedestrian connectivity: maximum block perimeter 400m, mid-block pedestrian passages every 100m
- Required retail: ground-floor retail on all blocks within 200m of station entrance
- Bicycle: secure indoor bicycle parking required, bike-share station within 50m of each station entrance
- Building orientation: primary entrances on streets leading to station

### SD-Historic (Historic Preservation District)

**Intent**: Preserve and enhance areas with significant architectural, cultural, or historical character.

**Modified Standards**:
- Demolition: demolition of contributing structures requires Historic Preservation Commission approval
- Alterations: exterior alterations to contributing structures require Certificate of Appropriateness
- New construction: must be compatible with historic context in height, scale, massing, setback, materials, and fenestration rhythm (but need not be historicist in style)
- Height: may not exceed the average height of contributing structures within one block
- Setback: must match the prevailing setback of contributing structures on the block face
- Materials: primary facade materials must be consistent with the historic palette of the district (masonry, wood clapboard, stucco, stone)
- References: Secretary of the Interior's Standards for the Treatment of Historic Properties, local historic district design guidelines

### SD-Innovation / Maker (Innovation District)

**Intent**: Support creative economy, light manufacturing, research and development, and maker spaces with flexible standards that accommodate non-traditional building uses and configurations.

**Modified Standards**:
- Uses: light manufacturing, fabrication, artisan production, R&D laboratories, co-working, startup incubator, live-work, gallery/showroom all permitted by right
- Building height: per underlying zone, but higher ground-floor ceilings (4.5-6.0m) permitted for equipment and flexibility
- Setback: flexible, may include loading/service areas visible from street if well-designed
- Parking: reduced, shared parking emphasized
- Signage: more permissive signage (murals, public art as signage, neon) to encourage creative identity
- Noise: higher ambient noise thresholds than residential zones (to accommodate fabrication)

## 5. Block and Connectivity Standards

### Maximum Block Dimensions

| Transect Zone | Max Block Perimeter | Max Block Face Length | Min Intersection Density | Notes |
|--------------|--------------------|--------------------|------------------------|-------|
| T3 | 500-600m | 150-200m | 40 intersections/sq km | Grid or modified grid |
| T4 | 400-500m | 120-150m | 60 intersections/sq km | Grid strongly preferred |
| T5 | 450-550m | 130-160m | 60+ intersections/sq km | Grid, may have mid-block passages |
| T6 | 500-600m | 150-200m | 50+ intersections/sq km | Grid, mid-block passages required for blocks >150m |

### Mid-Block Passage Requirements

When a block face exceeds 120m (T4-T5) or 150m (T6), a mid-block pedestrian passage is required.

**Passage Standards**:
- Width: 3.0-6.0m clear
- Height: open to sky or covered (if covered, minimum 4.5m clear height)
- Lighting: minimum 10 lux at floor level
- Activation: ground-floor retail, galleries, or display windows along passage
- Surface: high-quality paving, level with adjacent sidewalks
- No vehicles (except emergency access)
- Must connect two parallel streets (through-block connection)

### Alley / Rear Lane Standards

Alleys provide service access, parking access, and utility routing behind buildings, keeping the primary street frontage free of driveways, loading docks, and utility infrastructure.

**Standards**:
- Paved width: 4.0-6.0m (one-way: 3.0-4.0m)
- ROW width: 5.0-8.0m (allows utilities and minor landscape)
- Surface: asphalt, concrete, or permeable pavers
- Turning radius at intersections: 4.5-7.5m (for delivery trucks)
- Lighting: minimum 3 lux, shielded downlights on building faces or pole-mounted
- Drainage: center gutter or edge gutter to storm system
- Permitted uses: rear-loaded garage access, loading, utility meters, trash/recycling enclosures, accessory dwelling unit access
- Prohibited: habitable rooms opening directly onto alley without setback (minimum 1.5m setback for windows)

## 6. Density and Intensity Standards by Community Type

### Traditional Neighborhood Development (TND)

- Area: 16-80 ha (40-200 acres)
- Population: 1,500-6,000 residents
- Dwelling units: 500-2,000
- Center (T5): 5-10% of area, 30-50% of non-residential floor area
- General (T4): 30-50% of area
- Edge (T3): 30-50% of area
- Civic (parks, plazas, greens): 10-20% of area
- Walk to center: max 400m (5 minutes) from any point in the neighborhood

### Transit-Oriented Development (TOD)

- Area: 20-60 ha within 800m of station
- Population: 5,000-15,000 residents
- Dwelling units: 2,000-6,000
- Core (T6): 5-10% of area (within 200m of station)
- Center (T5): 15-25% of area (200-400m from station)
- General (T4): 30-40% of area (400-800m from station)
- Edge (T3): 10-20% of area (periphery)
- Walk to station: max 800m (10 minutes) from any point in the TOD

### Regional Center Development (RCD)

- Area: 40-200 ha
- Population: 10,000-50,000 (residents + workers)
- Mixed-use: significant employment base (office, institutional, commercial)
- Core (T6): 10-20% of area
- Center (T5): 20-30% of area
- General (T4): 20-30% of area
- Civic and open space: 15-25% of area
- Served by regional transit (rail, BRT, express bus)
