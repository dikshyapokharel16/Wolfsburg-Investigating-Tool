# ITDP TOD Standard v3.0 -- Complete Scoring Reference

This reference document provides the complete scoring methodology for the
Institute for Transportation and Development Policy (ITDP) TOD Standard v3.0.
It covers all 8 principles, each indicator within those principles, the
calculation methodology for each indicator, threshold values, and specific
design strategies to maximize scores.

---

## Overview

The ITDP TOD Standard evaluates transit-oriented developments on a 0-100 point
scale across 8 principles. It is the most widely used international benchmark
for TOD quality and has been applied to developments on every inhabited
continent.

### Scoring Summary

| Principle | Maximum Points | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1. WALK | 15 | 15% |
| 2. CYCLE | 5 | 5% |
| 3. CONNECT | 15 | 15% |
| 4. TRANSIT | 5 | 5% |
| 5. MIX | 15 | 15% |
| 6. DENSIFY | 15 | 15% |
| 7. COMPACT | 10 | 10% |
| 8. SHIFT | 20 | 20% |
| **TOTAL** | **100** | **100%** |

### Award Thresholds

| Level | Score | Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| **Gold** | 85+ | Score 85 or above AND no single principle scores zero AND meets mandatory deductions criteria |
| **Silver** | 70 - 84 | Score 70 or above AND no more than one principle scores zero |
| **Bronze** | 55 - 69 | Score 55 or above |
| **Not Rated** | Below 55 | Does not meet minimum threshold |

### Mandatory Deductions

Regardless of total score, the following conditions result in automatic
deductions or disqualification:

- **Superblock pattern**: if more than 20% of blocks exceed 500m perimeter, deduct 10 points
- **No pedestrian crossings**: if any arterial within the study area lacks safe pedestrian crossings within 200m spacing, deduct 5 points
- **Gated community**: if the development is gated (restricts public access to streets), automatic disqualification
- **Segregated affordable housing**: if affordable units are separated from market-rate by design or location, deduct 5 points

## Principle 1: WALK (0-15 points)

### Indicator 1.1: Pedestrian Network Completeness (0-3 points)

**Definition**: The percentage of streets within the study area that have
continuous, unobstructed sidewalks on both sides.

**Calculation**:
```
Completeness = (Length of streets with sidewalks on both sides) / (Total street length) x 100%
```

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 3 pts | 100% completeness |
| 2 pts | 90-99% completeness |
| 1 pt | 75-89% completeness |
| 0 pts | Below 75% completeness |

**Design Strategies to Maximize**:
- Conduct a gap analysis of the existing pedestrian network within the catchment
- Prioritize completing sidewalk gaps on routes between the development and the transit station
- Install sidewalks on both sides of every new street; minimum 2.0m clear width
- Where ROW is constrained, provide a shared surface (woonerf) rather than no sidewalk
- Ensure continuity across driveways and intersections (pedestrian has priority over turning vehicles)

### Indicator 1.2: Block Size (0-3 points)

**Definition**: The percentage of blocks within the study area with perimeters
less than 250m.

**Calculation**:
```
Small Block Ratio = (Number of blocks with perimeter < 250m) / (Total number of blocks) x 100%
```

Note: A "block" is defined as an area enclosed by public streets or paths that
are accessible to pedestrians. Private driveways and internal roads within a
superblock do not count as block-defining edges.

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 3 pts | 90%+ of blocks < 250m perimeter |
| 2 pts | 75-89% of blocks < 250m perimeter |
| 1 pt | 50-74% of blocks < 250m perimeter |
| 0 pts | Below 50% of blocks < 250m perimeter |

**Design Strategies to Maximize**:
- Target block dimensions of 60m x 60m (240m perimeter) as the default module
- Maximum block dimension: 120m in any direction; if unavoidable, provide mid-block pedestrian passage
- Insert new pedestrian streets or passages through existing superblocks
- Use pedestrian-only cut-throughs (minimum 3m wide, publicly accessible, well-lit) to break up large blocks
- Align block edges with desire lines to transit station entrances

### Indicator 1.3: Crossing Safety (0-3 points)

**Definition**: Quality and frequency of pedestrian crossings at intersections
and mid-block locations.

**Assessment Criteria**:
- All intersections have marked crosswalks
- Crosswalks are raised or signalized on streets with speed >30 km/h
- Pedestrian refuge islands on streets wider than 12m (4+ lanes)
- Maximum crossing distance without refuge: 12m
- Crossing spacing on arterials: maximum 100m
- Accessible curb ramps at every crossing

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 3 pts | All criteria met at 90%+ of crossings |
| 2 pts | All criteria met at 70-89% of crossings |
| 1 pt | Most criteria met, significant gaps in 2+ categories |
| 0 pts | Widespread deficiencies in crossing safety |

**Design Strategies to Maximize**:
- Raised crosswalks at every intersection within 400m of station (slows traffic, enhances visibility)
- Continental (high-visibility) crosswalk markings throughout the TOD area
- Pedestrian signals with countdown timers at all signalized intersections
- Leading Pedestrian Interval (LPI) of 5-7 seconds at all signals
- Curb extensions at every crossing to reduce crossing distance and improve sight lines
- Pedestrian refuge islands (minimum 1.8m wide) on all crossings of 4+ lane roads

### Indicator 1.4: Sidewalk Width and Quality (0-3 points)

**Definition**: Minimum clear width and surface quality of sidewalks.

**Assessment Criteria**:
- Minimum clear width (obstruction-free): 2.0m on local streets, 3.0m on commercial streets
- Surface condition: smooth, even, no trip hazards, well-maintained
- Cross-slope: maximum 2% for accessibility
- Material: durable, non-slip, well-drained
- Vertical obstructions (poles, signs, trees) are in the furnishing zone, not the clear zone

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 3 pts | 90%+ of sidewalks meet all criteria |
| 2 pts | 70-89% meet all criteria |
| 1 pt | 50-69% meet all criteria |
| 0 pts | Below 50% meet criteria |

**Design Strategies to Maximize**:
- Design sidewalks with distinct zones: frontage, clear, furnishing (see street design skill)
- Place all vertical elements (trees, lights, signs, furniture) in the furnishing zone
- Use cast-in-place concrete with broom finish for the clear zone (durable, accessible, affordable)
- Install detectable warning surfaces at all curb ramps (truncated domes, contrasting color)
- Program regular maintenance: repair cracks and heaving within 30 days, sweep weekly

### Indicator 1.5: Pedestrian Comfort (0-3 points)

**Definition**: Environmental quality of the pedestrian experience including
shade, weather protection, lighting, and frontage activity.

**Assessment Criteria**:
- Shade coverage: 40%+ of primary pedestrian routes shaded (trees, canopies, arcades)
- Lighting: minimum 10 lux on all pedestrian routes, 30 lux at crossings
- Active frontages: 60%+ of ground-floor facades on primary routes have active uses
- Weather protection: covered waiting areas at transit stops, arcade or canopy coverage on commercial streets
- Visual interest: building facades articulated at 6-8m intervals, no blank walls exceeding 15m

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 3 pts | All five criteria met |
| 2 pts | Four of five criteria met |
| 1 pt | Three of five criteria met |
| 0 pts | Two or fewer criteria met |

**Design Strategies to Maximize**:
- Plant street trees at 8-10m intervals with species selected for large canopy spread (minimum 6m)
- Require active ground-floor uses (retail, F&B, services) on all streets within 200m of station
- Install pedestrian-scale lighting (4-5m poles) separate from roadway lighting
- Mandate weather protection (arcades, canopies, or awnings) along primary retail frontages
- Prohibit blank walls: where active use is not feasible, require public art, green walls, or display windows

## Principle 2: CYCLE (0-5 points)

### Indicator 2.1: Cycling Network (0-2 points)

**Definition**: Availability and quality of cycling infrastructure connecting
the development to the transit station and surrounding destinations.

**Assessment Criteria**:
- Protected bike lanes (physically separated) on all collector and arterial streets
- Low-stress cycling routes (shared streets, traffic-calmed locals) on all other streets
- Direct cycling routes to station (route directness ratio <1.5)
- Network connectivity: no gaps or missing links in the cycling network

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 2 pts | Complete protected cycling network within the study area |
| 1 pt | Partial network with some gaps but functional routes to station |
| 0 pts | No dedicated cycling infrastructure |

**Design Strategies to Maximize**:
- Install protected bike lanes (minimum 2.0m one-way, 3.0m two-way) on all roads classified as collector or higher
- On local streets, use traffic calming to achieve 30 km/h or lower (comfortable for shared cycling)
- Eliminate pinch points and conflict zones at intersections (protected intersection design)
- Provide safe, signalized bike crossings at arterial roads
- Extend cycling network beyond the TOD boundary to connect to the city-wide network

### Indicator 2.2: Bicycle Parking (0-2 points)

**Definition**: Quantity and quality of bicycle parking at the transit station
and within the development.

**Standards**:
- Station parking: minimum 1 space per 100 daily boardings, at least 50 spaces for metro
- Residential: 1 secure, covered space per unit + 1 visitor space per 10 units
- Office: 1 space per 200 m2 GFA (minimum), with showers and lockers
- Retail: 1 space per 250 m2 GFA at building entrances
- Quality: covered, secure, well-lit, close to entrances, accessible design

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 2 pts | Meets all quantity and quality standards |
| 1 pt | Meets quantity standards but quality deficient, or vice versa |
| 0 pts | Significantly below standards |

**Design Strategies to Maximize**:
- Over-provide bicycle parking (150% of minimum) to accommodate growing cycling demand
- Station bicycle parking: secure cage or automated parking system, 24-hour access, CCTV monitored
- Residential bicycle parking: ground-floor secure room with direct exterior access, electrical outlets
- Provide cargo bike and adaptive bike parking (larger spaces) at 10% of supply
- Install e-bike charging at 10% of parking spaces

### Indicator 2.3: Bike-Share (0-1 point)

**Definition**: Availability of a bike-share system within the TOD area.

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 1 pt | Bike-share station within 300m of transit stop, minimum 15 docks |
| 0 pts | No bike-share access |

**Design Strategies to Maximize**:
- Negotiate with bike-share operator for a station adjacent to the transit stop
- Provide ground-level space (minimum 20 m2) in a visible, accessible location for the docking station
- If no system-wide bike-share exists, implement a development-scale bike-share program
- Consider dockless bike-share with designated parking zones if dock-based is unavailable

## Principle 3: CONNECT (0-15 points)

### Indicator 3.1: Intersection Density (0-5 points)

**Definition**: Number of street intersections per square kilometer within
the study area.

**Calculation**:
```
Intersection Density = (Total number of 3-way or more intersections) / (Study area in km2)
```

Count only publicly accessible intersections where at least two streets meet
(T-intersections, 4-way, roundabouts). Exclude private driveways.

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 5 pts | 120+ intersections per km2 |
| 4 pts | 100-119 intersections per km2 |
| 3 pts | 80-99 intersections per km2 |
| 2 pts | 60-79 intersections per km2 |
| 1 pt | 40-59 intersections per km2 |
| 0 pts | Below 40 intersections per km2 |

**Benchmark**: Barcelona's Eixample has approximately 120 intersections/km2;
Manhattan's grid has approximately 200; typical North American suburban
development has 20-40.

**Design Strategies to Maximize**:
- Grid or modified grid street pattern with 70-90m block spacing
- Break up existing superblocks with new public streets or pedestrian passages
- Ensure all new streets connect to the existing network at both ends (no dead ends)
- Count pedestrian-only passages as intersections if they connect two public streets

### Indicator 3.2: Small Blocks (0-4 points)

**Definition**: Percentage of blocks with perimeter less than 400m.

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 4 pts | 90%+ of blocks < 400m perimeter |
| 3 pts | 75-89% of blocks < 400m perimeter |
| 2 pts | 60-74% of blocks < 400m perimeter |
| 1 pt | 40-59% of blocks < 400m perimeter |
| 0 pts | Below 40% of blocks < 400m perimeter |

Note: This indicator uses a 400m perimeter threshold (more lenient than
Indicator 1.2's 250m), reflecting the connectivity principle's focus on
network structure rather than pedestrian comfort.

### Indicator 3.3: Route Directness (0-3 points)

**Definition**: Ratio of actual walking distance to straight-line distance
for typical origin-destination pairs within the study area.

**Calculation**:
```
Route Directness Ratio = Average (Network distance / Euclidean distance)
for 20+ randomly sampled origin-destination pairs within the study area
```

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 3 pts | Average ratio < 1.3 |
| 2 pts | Average ratio 1.3 - 1.5 |
| 1 pt | Average ratio 1.5 - 1.8 |
| 0 pts | Average ratio > 1.8 |

**Benchmark**: A perfect grid has a ratio of approximately 1.27 (the "Manhattan
distance" factor). Cul-de-sac patterns typically score 1.6-2.5.

### Indicator 3.4: Connectivity Index (0-3 points)

**Definition**: Ratio of links (street segments) to nodes (intersections) in
the street network.

**Calculation**:
```
Connectivity Index = Number of links / Number of nodes
```

A higher ratio indicates more alternative routes (fewer dead ends, more
connections).

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 3 pts | Index > 1.6 |
| 2 pts | Index 1.4 - 1.6 |
| 1 pt | Index 1.2 - 1.4 |
| 0 pts | Index < 1.2 |

**Benchmark**: A perfect 4-way grid has an index of approximately 2.0. A tree-
like cul-de-sac network approaches 1.0.

**Scoring**:

## Principle 5: MIX (0-15 points)

### Indicator 5.1: Complementary Uses (0-5 points)

**Definition**: Presence of complementary land use categories within the
study area.

**Categories**: residential, office/employment, retail/commercial, civic/
institutional, recreation/open space, accommodation (hotel).

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 5 pts | 5+ categories present, well-distributed |
| 4 pts | 4 categories present |
| 3 pts | 3 categories present |
| 2 pts | 2 categories present |
| 0 pts | Mono-functional (1 category) |

### Indicator 5.2: Housing Type Diversity (0-3 points)

**Definition**: Range of housing types and unit sizes within the development.

**Types**: studio/efficiency, 1-bedroom, 2-bedroom, 3+ bedroom, townhouse/
rowhouse, live-work, senior/accessible, co-living.

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 3 pts | 5+ housing types |
| 2 pts | 3-4 housing types |
| 1 pt | 2 housing types |
| 0 pts | 1 housing type (monoculture) |

### Indicator 5.3: Affordable Housing (0-4 points)

**Definition**: Percentage of residential units affordable to households
earning 60% or less of the Area Median Income (AMI).

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 4 pts | 30%+ affordable |
| 3 pts | 20-29% affordable |
| 2 pts | 15-19% affordable |
| 1 pt | 10-14% affordable |
| 0 pts | Below 10% affordable |

**Deduction**: If affordable units are physically separated (different
building, different entrance, different finishes), deduct 2 points.

### Indicator 5.4: Income Diversity (0-3 points)

**Definition**: Range of income levels served by the unit mix and pricing
structure.

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 3 pts | Units serving 4+ income brackets (low, moderate, middle, upper) |
| 2 pts | Units serving 3 income brackets |
| 1 pt | Units serving 2 income brackets |
| 0 pts | Single income bracket served |

## Principle 6: DENSIFY (0-15 points)

### Indicator 6.1: Residential Density (0-5 points)

**Definition**: Residential population density within the study area.

**Calculation**:
```
Residential Density = (Total residents) / (Study area in km2)
```

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 5 pts | 25,000+ people per km2 |
| 4 pts | 20,000-24,999 per km2 |
| 3 pts | 15,000-19,999 per km2 |
| 2 pts | 10,000-14,999 per km2 |
| 1 pt | 5,000-9,999 per km2 |
| 0 pts | Below 5,000 per km2 |

**Benchmark**: Paris: 21,000/km2; Barcelona: 16,000/km2; Singapore new towns:
15,000-25,000/km2; typical North American suburb: 1,000-3,000/km2.

### Indicator 6.2: Non-Residential Density (0-5 points)

**Definition**: Employment and visitor density (daytime population excluding
residents).

**Calculation**:
```
Non-Residential Density = (Total employees + daily visitors) / (Study area in km2)
```

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 5 pts | 25,000+ per km2 |
| 4 pts | 15,000-24,999 per km2 |
| 3 pts | 10,000-14,999 per km2 |
| 2 pts | 5,000-9,999 per km2 |
| 1 pt | 2,500-4,999 per km2 |
| 0 pts | Below 2,500 per km2 |

### Indicator 6.3: Combined Density Support for Transit (0-5 points)

**Definition**: Whether combined residential and employment density is
sufficient to support the transit service level.

**Thresholds by Transit Mode**:
| Transit Mode | Minimum Combined Density for Score |
|---|---|
| Metro / Heavy Rail | 20,000+ combined/km2 for 5 pts |
| LRT / Tram | 12,000+ combined/km2 for 5 pts |
| BRT | 10,000+ combined/km2 for 5 pts |
| Frequent Bus | 7,000+ combined/km2 for 5 pts |

## Principle 7: COMPACT (0-10 points)

### Indicator 7.1: Contiguity (0-4 points)

**Definition**: Percentage of the development perimeter that is contiguous
with existing urbanized land.

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 4 pts | 90%+ contiguous |
| 3 pts | 70-89% contiguous |
| 2 pts | 50-69% contiguous |
| 1 pt | 30-49% contiguous |
| 0 pts | Below 30% (leapfrog development) |

### Indicator 7.2: Infill Priority (0-3 points)

**Definition**: Proportion of the development area that is infill (previously
developed or underutilized land) versus greenfield.

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 3 pts | 90%+ infill |
| 2 pts | 60-89% infill |
| 1 pt | 30-59% infill |
| 0 pts | Below 30% infill (predominantly greenfield) |

### Indicator 7.3: Urban Fabric Continuity (0-3 points)

**Definition**: How well the new development extends and completes the
existing urban fabric (street pattern, building frontages, public spaces).

**Assessment Criteria**:
- New streets extend existing streets and connect to the surrounding grid
- Building frontages align with existing setback patterns at development edges
- Public spaces are connected to existing open space networks
- No blank walls, fences, or barriers at the development boundary

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 3 pts | Seamless integration with surrounding urban fabric |
| 2 pts | Mostly integrated with minor disconnections |
| 1 pt | Partially integrated; significant barriers or discontinuities |
| 0 pts | Isolated; gated, fenced, or walled off from surroundings |

## Principle 8: SHIFT (0-20 points)

This principle carries the highest weight (20%) because reducing car
dependence is the most impactful outcome of well-designed TOD.

### Indicator 8.1: Parking Supply (0-5 points)

**Definition**: Off-street parking supply relative to the number of
residential units and commercial floor area.

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 5 pts | 0.0 - 0.3 spaces per residential unit AND < 1.0/100 m2 commercial |
| 4 pts | 0.3 - 0.5 per unit AND 1.0-2.0/100 m2 |
| 3 pts | 0.5 - 0.7 per unit AND 2.0-3.0/100 m2 |
| 2 pts | 0.7 - 1.0 per unit AND 3.0-4.0/100 m2 |
| 1 pt | 1.0 - 1.5 per unit OR 4.0-5.0/100 m2 |
| 0 pts | Above 1.5 per unit OR above 5.0/100 m2 |

### Indicator 8.2: Parking Visibility (0-3 points)

**Definition**: Whether off-street parking is hidden from the public realm.

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 3 pts | All parking underground or fully wrapped with active uses; no parking visible from any street |
| 2 pts | Most parking hidden; minor visible parking structures with architectural treatment |
| 1 pt | Significant visible parking structures or small surface lots |
| 0 pts | Dominant surface parking or highly visible parking garages |

### Indicator 8.3: Traffic Calming (0-4 points)

**Definition**: Extent and effectiveness of traffic calming measures on local
streets within the study area.

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 4 pts | Comprehensive calming: all local streets 30 km/h or below, physical measures at every block |
| 3 pts | Extensive calming: most local streets calmed, speed limit 30 km/h |
| 2 pts | Moderate calming: some streets calmed, 30 km/h zones present |
| 1 pt | Limited calming: speed limit reduced but few physical measures |
| 0 pts | No traffic calming |

### Indicator 8.4: Car-Free Zones (0-4 points)

**Definition**: Presence and size of car-free or car-restricted zones within
the study area.

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 4 pts | Car-free zone of 200m+ radius around station, including main commercial streets |
| 3 pts | Car-free zone of 100-200m radius or car-free main street |
| 2 pts | Pedestrian-priority streets (shared surface, vehicles permitted at <10 km/h) |
| 1 pt | Part-time car-free areas (weekend closures, event streets) |
| 0 pts | No car-free or car-restricted areas |

### Indicator 8.5: TDM Program (0-4 points)

**Definition**: Presence and comprehensiveness of a Transportation Demand
Management program.

**TDM Elements**:
1. Car-share: dedicated spaces, partnership with operator
2. Bike-share: on-site or within 300m
3. Transit pass subsidy: employer-provided transit benefits
4. Real-time multimodal information: app or displays showing transit, bike-share, car-share availability
5. Parking pricing: market-rate, unbundled from housing and office leases
6. Carpool/vanpool program: matching service, priority parking
7. Delivery consolidation: shared loading, last-mile cargo bikes
8. Mobility hub: co-located car-share, bike-share, e-scooter, transit information at a single point

**Scoring**:
| Score | Threshold |
|---|---|
| 4 pts | 7-8 TDM elements in place |
| 3 pts | 5-6 TDM elements |
| 2 pts | 3-4 TDM elements |
| 1 pt | 1-2 TDM elements |
| 0 pts | No TDM program |

## Strategies for Achieving Gold

To achieve Gold (85+), a project must excel across all principles with no zeros.
The following strategies address the most commonly deficient areas:

### Common Failure Points

1. **SHIFT scores low**: parking supply too high. Reduce to 0.3 spaces/unit or below within 400m. Implement comprehensive TDM.
2. **CONNECT scores low**: street network inherited from suburban context. Insert new streets and pedestrian passages to increase intersection density above 100/km2.
3. **MIX scores low**: affordable housing below 20%. Use inclusionary zoning, land trusts, or cross-subsidy from commercial development.
4. **DENSIFY scores low**: density not concentrated near station. Redistribute program to front-load Zone A and B parcels.
5. **WALK scores low**: pedestrian environment uncomfortable. Invest in shade (trees + canopies), lighting, and active ground floors.

### Point Optimization Strategy

Focus on the highest-weighted principles first:
1. **SHIFT (20 pts)**: aggressive parking reduction and TDM yield the highest marginal points
2. **WALK (15 pts)**: complete the pedestrian network and invest in comfort
